# LW-02 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS

## Task 1 - Model Answer

The audience is the supervising partner. The purpose is to produce a internal memo that helps the reader understand the position, practical risk, and next step. The response should be concise, supervisor-facing, risk-aware, and neutral and should not go beyond the evidence.

## Task 2 - Model Answer

Key facts include: Partner Jonah Reed asks for a short memo on what the firm can safely say in a reference for former payroll manager Keira Dunn; The requesting employer asks for confirmation of Keira’s employment dates, job title, reason for leaving, performance, reliability, and whether there are any concerns about trust or accuracy; Keira resigned on 18 April after receiving informal feedback about payroll errors. The facts should be presented in a logical order and tied back to the main written response rather than listed without analysis.

## Task 3 - Model Answer

The issues are factual uncertainty, communication risk, evidence gaps, and the need to write in a way that is useful without overstating the legal position. The student should identify both the supported concerns and the limits of the evidence.

## Task 4 - Model Answer

The client objective is to produce a balanced internal memo recommending safe wording for a reference response. The written response should advance that objective while preserving credibility, avoiding unnecessary escalation, and leaving room for supervisor review.

## Task 5 - Model Answer

The communication must explain the supported facts, the practical significance of those facts, the points that remain uncertain, and the recommended next steps. It should be specific enough to be useful and simple enough for the intended reader.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The student should avoid unsupported allegations, definitive conclusions not proved by the exhibits, unnecessary admissions, threats that have not been approved, and language that may damage credibility. In this scenario, avoid: overstating misconduct that was not concluded; using emotional internal comments as fact; approving an overly glowing reference; including sensitive personal information unnecessarily.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Important caveats include the lack of complete evidence, unresolved factual disputes, and the need for supervisor review before making stronger assertions. The communication should distinguish known facts from allegations, assumptions, and points requiring follow-up.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible structure is: short introduction, confirmed factual summary, key issue or risk, caveats/missing evidence, practical recommendation, and next steps. The tone should be concise, supervisor-facing, risk-aware, and neutral.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Key points should include: the purpose and audience of the memo; which facts are confirmed and which remain internal impressions; risk of misleadingly positive or unsupported negative statements; a recommended neutral response approach. These should be drafted in complete, audience-appropriate sentences rather than as internal notes.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final communication should be accurate, concise, and usable. It should address the reader directly, include only supported facts, make uncertainty clear, and end with an actionable next step.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Internal memo - Keira Dunn reference request

Purpose: The firm needs a careful response to a reference request about Keira Dunn. The safest approach is to provide accurate, limited, fact-based information and avoid unsupported positive or negative conclusions.

Confirmed facts: Keira was employed as payroll manager. She resigned on 18 April. Internal documents record payroll issues, including late pension uploads and corrected overtime calculations. No disciplinary finding was made. The payroll review identifies several possible causes, including systems migration problems and staff shortages. Keira also raised concerns about training.

Risk points: It would be unsafe to state that Keira was excellent and fully reliable if the firm knows there were unresolved performance concerns. It would also be unsafe to state or imply dishonesty, misconduct, or untrustworthiness when no formal finding was made and the evidence is mixed. Informal comments such as “I would not trust her with payroll again” should not be repeated as fact.

Recommended response: The reference should be brief and neutral. It can confirm employment dates, role, and broad duties. If the firm chooses to answer performance questions, wording should be carefully limited, for example: “During Ms Dunn’s employment, some payroll processing issues were reviewed. No disciplinary finding was made before her resignation.” The firm should avoid inviting an informal call for “the real story” unless a partner has approved exactly what may be said.

Next steps: Ask HR to confirm dates, job title, and any reference policy. Obtain partner approval before sending any comment beyond dates and role.

## AI Skill 1 - Model Answer

A good student may use AI to propose a structure, but should revise it so that it matches the audience, the factual record, and the purpose of the communication. The student should check that the AI has not introduced new facts or legal conclusions.

## AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

A good student should identify and remove overstatement, blame language, premature threats, unsupported admissions, and vague drafting. AI suggestions should be treated as drafting options, not final judgment.

## AI Skill 3 - Model Answer

A good student should compare the AI fact/assumption list against all six exhibits. Confirmed facts should be separated from disputed points, inferred points, and missing evidence. Any hallucinated or unsupported AI output should be corrected.

## AI Section Completion Standard - Tutor Guidance

# AI Simulation Agent Brief

Simulation Type: Clarification call before writing or live feedback on a written message.

Agent Role: Supervisor or client contact, depending on platform setup.

Agent Persona: A time-pressured stakeholder who wants a useful internal memo and is concerned about a former employee reference request after unresolved performance concerns.

Simulation Purpose: The student practises clarifying audience, purpose, tone, missing information, and unsafe wording before finalising the written communication.

Opening Statement: Thanks for looking at this. I need a clear written response, but I do not want us to say anything that creates unnecessary risk. What do you think we can safely say?

## Core Facts the Agent Knows

A supervising partner needs an internal memo before responding to a reference request about a former payroll manager. The business wants to be helpful, but internal documents include performance concerns, an unresolved complaint, and a draft reference that may be too positive.

The final communication must be practical and evidence-led.

There are missing or disputed facts that should not be glossed over.

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character and do not give model answers.

Ask the student to justify wording by reference to the exhibits.

Push back if the student uses unsupported allegations or promises.

Reward clear, measured, audience-aware communication.

Do not invent new facts beyond the exhibits and brief.
