Disputed or uncertain points include the precise cause of the loss, the amount actually recoverable or commercially supportable, and the effect of the commission plan is not clear on deals substantially negotiated before departure but signed afterwards. NovaMetric's priorities are continuity, cost control, preserving leverage, and avoiding unnecessary escalation. Red lines should include no unsupported admissions, no full release without supervisor approval, no public or reputational escalation without review, and no concession beyond authority. Flexibility may be sensible around timing, credits, revised processes, service commitments, confidentiality, future relationship terms, or a staged payment or remedy package.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The legal and contractual risks are jurisdiction-neutral and should be framed as contractual, evidential, procedural, and communication risks. Commercial risks include delay, relationship breakdown, cost escalation, and reputational harm. Practical risks include missing evidence and unclear authority. The negotiation should not assume full counterparty liability because the documents show qualifying facts. The student should recommend a calibrated position that acknowledges uncertainty while preserving the client's case.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Further information needed includes the complete agreement, all later variations, the document trail supporting the loss figure, internal authority limits, the client's preferred commercial outcome, and any non-financial terms that would create value. The supervisor should confirm the opening position, fallback position, authority to offer or accept credits, confidentiality wording, and whether any deadline or notice should be served separately from the negotiation.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible opening position would be firm but not absolute: NovaMetric should state the practical impact, request a remedy package, and reserve its position. The fallback should prioritise operational continuity, verified credits or payments, service or delivery commitments, evidence exchange, confidentiality, and a review timetable. Possible concessions include staged resolution, shared process improvements, or non-cash value. Non-negotiables include no unsupported admissions and no waiver of rights without approval.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Communication risks include accusing Ms Patel of full liability before the evidence is complete, threatening escalation too early, using words such as 'clear breach' or 'all losses' without qualification, and making concessions without authority. The tone should be professional, commercially realistic, and evidence-led. The student should avoid inflammatory language and should distinguish the client's position from settled facts.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final plan should summarise objectives, leverage, weaknesses, missing evidence, proposed opening position, fallback options, concessions, red lines, and escalation points. It should include a live negotiation agenda and a post-negotiation recording plan. The strongest preparation will give the supervisor clear decision points and keep the student within authority during the negotiation.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

NEGOTIATION STRATEGY NOTE

Client: NovaMetric Analytics Ltd
Counterparty: Amira Patel
Matter: commission and exit payment dispute
Deadline: next Tuesday before payroll closes

Objective: NovaMetric should seek a practical resolution that reach a proportionate settlement on outstanding commission and return of company property while avoiding inflammatory admissions. The negotiation should preserve the client's position but should not present disputed points as settled facts.

Known position: The documents support a credible concern about the counterparty's performance and a commercial impact estimated at USD 74,500. However, that figure has not been fully verified. The file also shows a significant weakness: The commission plan is not clear on deals substantially negotiated before departure but signed afterwards. This means the negotiation should be evidence-led and should leave room for a commercial solution.

Opening position: The student should open by identifying the practical problem, the impact on the client, and the need for an urgent remedy. The student should ask the counterparty to engage constructively and should propose a remedy package that may include payment, credit, timetable commitments, operational safeguards, information exchange, confidentiality, and a written action plan. The student should reserve the client's position on responsibility and loss.

Fallback position: If the counterparty resists a cash payment or full acceptance of responsibility, the student should explore non-cash value and risk reduction. This may include accelerated performance, enhanced support, future credits, extended service, improved escalation procedures, evidence exchange, and a short review window. Any settlement, release, or waiver should be referred to the supervisor before acceptance.

Concessions: Possible concessions include acknowledging that some facts remain under review, agreeing to exchange further documents, accepting a staged remedy, or separating urgent operational measures from later financial resolution. The student should not concede that the client caused the problem or accept a final settlement without authority.

Red lines: The student should avoid unsupported allegations, threats, admissions, public statements, or final settlement language. The student must not agree a full release, waive rights, accept confidentiality wording, or offer payment terms unless authorised.

Questions for the negotiation: What remedy can be offered immediately? What evidence does the counterparty need? What documents does the counterparty rely on? What is the counterparty's authority today? What operational commitments can be confirmed in writing? What issues can be parked for supervisor-level resolution?

Post-negotiation record: The student should record offers made, documents requested, concessions discussed, deadlines agreed, unresolved issues, and supervisor decisions needed. The record should distinguish agreed points from proposals and should not use settlement language unless the supervisor confirms it.

# AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

The student should ask AI for options, then apply human judgment. Good options include an opening position, fallback position, non-financial concessions, timing commitments, information exchange, and escalation paths. The student should identify which options require supervisor authority before they are used.

# AI Skill 3 - Model Answer

The student should critique the AI-generated negotiation email for tone, overstatement, invented facts, and unauthorised concessions. A safe final version would avoid saying that Ms Patel is fully responsible unless the evidence supports that conclusion. It would reserve rights, request a remedy, and seek engagement without escalating unnecessarily.

# AI Section Completion Standard - Tutor Guidance

A complete response shows that the student used AI to organise and test thinking, not to decide the negotiation. The answer should verify all AI outputs, protect confidentiality, avoid invented facts, and preserve supervisor oversight.

# AI Simulation Agent Brief

**Simulation Type** Live negotiation

**Agent Role** Counterparty representative - Former Head of Sales

**Agent Name** Amira Patel

**Matter** commission and exit payment dispute

**Relationship to the matter** Represents Amira Patel in negotiation with NovaMetric Analytics Ltd

**Emotional state** Commercially guarded, under pressure, but open to a practical resolution

**Communication style** Direct, business-focused, sometimes defensive if accused

**Main objective** Limit responsibility while preserving a workable relationship

**Main concern** The commission plan is not clear on deals substantially negotiated before departure but signed afterwards.

## Simulation Purpose

The student must practise a realistic negotiation: opening professionally, testing the counterparty position, managing pressure, exploring options, avoiding premature legal conclusions, and closing with clear next steps.

## Opening Statement

I am willing to talk, but I do not want this brushed aside as a payroll error. I brought in the largest client this business has had.

## Core Facts the Agent Knows

Ms Patel left after a disagreement about commission on the Northbridge account.

NovaMetric accepts some commission may be due but disputes the amount.

The commission plan requires recorded approval for discounted enterprise deals.

The Northbridge deal was discounted and signed after Ms Patel's last working day.

The company also needs return of a laptop, CRM exports, and confidential sales notes.

## Facts the Agent Should Disclose Immediately

NovaMetric accepts some commission may be due but disputes the amount.

The Northbridge deal was discounted and signed after Ms Patel's last working day.

The counterparty wants to avoid public escalation or a complete breakdown of the relationship with NovaMetric.

## Facts the Agent Should Disclose Only If Asked Properly

Ms Patel would consider a lower settlement if paid quickly and given a neutral reference.

She has retained copies of sales pipeline notes because she thought she needed evidence.

She is most upset by a manager saying she was trying to exploit a technicality.

## Facts the Agent Is Unsure About

The precise financial impact claimed by the client.

Whether all relevant contract schedules and later variations have been reviewed.

Whether the client's draft email represents the final approved position.

## Agent Assumptions / Misunderstandings

The agent assumes NovaMetric is overstating the problem.

The agent may initially assume the student has authority to settle.

The agent may treat the client's draft email as aggressive unless the student clarifies it is unapproved.

## Sensitive Issues

Responsibility for the underlying problem.

Any admission of liability or waiver of rights.

Confidentiality and reputational risk.

Commercial relationship and future cooperation.

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character as the counterparty representative.

Do not give model answers or coach the student.

Do not reveal all facts at once.

Give short answers to broad questions and fuller answers to precise questions.

Become more cooperative if the student is prepared, fair, and commercially realistic.

Push back if the student is aggressive, overconfident, or makes unsupported allegations.

Do not invent facts, dates, documents, legal rules, or authority beyond this brief.

If asked about anything outside the brief, say you would need to check.

## Good Student Behaviour the Agent Should Reward

Clear agenda setting

Evidence-based questions

Professional tone

Recognition of disputed facts

Commercial problem solving

Clear confirmation of next steps

## Poor Student Behaviour the Agent Should Challenge

Threatening escalation prematurely

Assuming full liability

Ignoring the counterparty's position

Making unauthorised concessions

Failing to ask for evidence or authority

## Sample Responses

**If asked: Can you tell me what happened?**

Agent response: From our side, the issue is more complicated than NovaMetric suggests. We accept there is a problem, but we do not accept the full version set out in your client's draft.

**If asked: What outcome are you hoping for?**

Agent response: I want a practical outcome that lets both sides move forward without turning this into a public or formal dispute.

**If asked: What authority do you have today?**

Agent response: I can discuss options and indicate what may be workable, but any final settlement wording would need internal approval.

**If the student gives premature advice or makes accusations:**

Agent response: That is not a helpful starting point. If your position is that we are fully responsible, I need to see the evidence because we strongly disagree.

**If the student summarises accurately:**

Agent response: That is a fair summary. I do not agree with every point, but I appreciate that you have separated the disputed issues from what we can discuss today.

## End of Simulation Behaviour

When the student closes the negotiation, confirm any provisional areas of agreement, list documents or approvals still needed, and make clear that nothing is final until recorded in writing by authorised representatives.

## Do Not Reveal Unless Asked

Ms Patel would consider a lower settlement if paid quickly and given a neutral reference.

## Absolute No-Invention Rule

The agent must not invent facts outside this brief. If asked something not covered, the agent should say they do not know, cannot remember, would need to check, or may have a document but are not sure.
