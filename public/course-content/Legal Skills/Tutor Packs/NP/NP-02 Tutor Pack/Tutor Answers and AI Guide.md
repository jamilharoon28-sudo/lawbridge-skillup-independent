### Tutor Use Note

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibit documents.

The model answers indicate the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

## Matter Snapshot for Tutors

A retailer missed a seasonal sales window after a supplier delivered packaging late. The supplier denies full responsibility and wants to preserve the relationship. The client wants compensation but also needs future supply.

> AI Extension Model Answers - Three Scenario Questions

> Tutor assessment focus Tutors should assess only three AI questions. Strong answers must be grounded in NP-02 - Preparing to Settle a Supplier Delay Dispute and the matching exhibit bundle, not generic AI risk language.

> - AI Task 1 - Human Skill First Strong answer identifies three priority issues from the exhibit bundle, explains why they matter for Negotiation Preparation, separates confirmed facts from gaps, and states what must be checked or escalated before AI is used.

> - AI Task 2 - AI Tool Usage and Critique Strong answer gives one safe prompt limited to NP-02 - Preparing to Settle a Supplier Delay Dispute; asks for structure, critique or drafting support only; requires primary practical output, risk or issue list, reflection or improvement note; prohibits invented facts or assumed law; and explains how the output will be checked against exhibits and source reliability.

> - AI Task 3 - Judgment and AI Supervision Under Pressure Strong answer explains what the student would accept, change or reject from AI output; keeps caveats visible; identifies missing evidence and approval points; and produces a supervisor-ready position grounded in the scenario.
