### Tutor Use Note

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibit documents.

The model answers indicate the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

## Matter Snapshot for Tutors

A client wants to buy an AI tool for internal document triage. The supplier pack includes a proposal, terms, security schedule and product FAQ. The client asks for a practical red-flag review before procurement approval.

## Required Output Guidance

Document review table

Document, issue, risk, evidence, recommendation and priority.

Evidence standard: Must cite document sections.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Red flag summary

Top five issues for the client.

Evidence standard: Must prioritise, not list everything equally.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Follow-up request list

Questions or documents needed from supplier.

Evidence standard: Each request must link to a risk.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

> AI Extension Model Answers - Three Scenario Questions

> Tutor assessment focus Tutors should assess only three AI questions. Strong answers must be grounded in DR-01 - Reviewing an AI Procurement Pack and the matching exhibit bundle, not generic AI risk language.

> - AI Task 1 - Human Skill First Strong answer identifies three priority issues from the exhibit bundle, explains why they matter for Document Review, separates confirmed facts from gaps, and states what must be checked or escalated before AI is used.

> - AI Task 2 - AI Tool Usage and Critique Strong answer gives one safe prompt limited to DR-01 - Reviewing an AI Procurement Pack; asks for structure, critique or drafting support only; requires primary practical output, risk or issue list, reflection or improvement note; prohibits invented facts or assumed law; and explains how the output will be checked against exhibits and source reliability.

> - AI Task 3 - Judgment and AI Supervision Under Pressure Strong answer explains what the student would accept, change or reject from AI output; keeps caveats visible; identifies missing evidence and approval points; and produces a supervisor-ready position grounded in the scenario.

| Common weaknesses to mark down |
| --- |
| Generic AI commentary; asking AI for the final answer; failure to cite or check exhibits; hiding uncertainty; no supervisor escalation where facts, law or approvals remain unresolved. |
