# LW-09 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS

## Task 1 - Model Answer

The audience is the anxious client. The purpose is to produce a case update email that helps the reader understand the position, practical risk, and next step. The response should be reassuring, clear, careful, and practical and should not go beyond the evidence.

## Task 2 - Model Answer

Key facts include: Priya owns 20 percent of Raman Bakery Ltd; The accounts summary shows revenue broadly stable but increased supplier costs and director expenses; Dividends were paid quarterly until November. The facts should be presented in a logical order and tied back to the main written response rather than listed without analysis.

## Task 3 - Model Answer

The issues are factual uncertainty, communication risk, evidence gaps, and the need to write in a way that is useful without overstating the legal position. The student should identify both the supported concerns and the limits of the evidence.

## Task 4 - Model Answer

The client objective is to write a client update that explains progress, evidence gaps, and next steps without inflaming the dispute. The written response should advance that objective while preserving credibility, avoiding unnecessary escalation, and leaving room for supervisor review.

## Task 5 - Model Answer

The communication must explain the supported facts, the practical significance of those facts, the points that remain uncertain, and the recommended next steps. It should be specific enough to be useful and simple enough for the intended reader.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The student should avoid unsupported allegations, definitive conclusions not proved by the exhibits, unnecessary admissions, threats that have not been approved, and language that may damage credibility. In this scenario, avoid: accusing family members of fraud without evidence; guaranteeing outcome; sending emotionally loaded wording; mixing family grievances with documented business issues.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Important caveats include the lack of complete evidence, unresolved factual disputes, and the need for supervisor review before making stronger assertions. The communication should distinguish known facts from allegations, assumptions, and points requiring follow-up.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible structure is: short introduction, confirmed factual summary, key issue or risk, caveats/missing evidence, practical recommendation, and next steps. The tone should be reassuring, clear, careful, and practical.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Key points should include: what documents have been received; what concerns are raised by incomplete accounts and messages; why further documents are needed; next steps for a controlled information request. These should be drafted in complete, audience-appropriate sentences rather than as internal notes.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final communication should be accurate, concise, and usable. It should address the reader directly, include only supported facts, make uncertainty clear, and end with an actionable next step.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Dear Priya,

Thank you for sending the accounts extract, dividend history, and message thread. I appreciate that this is stressful, especially because it involves family members as well as the business.

The documents raise legitimate questions, but they do not yet prove that money has been hidden or misused. The accounts extract shows stable revenue alongside increased supplier costs and director expenses. The dividend history shows that dividends stopped after November. However, we do not yet have the underlying invoices, bank statements, board minutes, dividend decisions, or director loan information needed to understand why dividends stopped.

The messages from your brother are hostile and unhelpful, particularly the comment that you will “get nothing” if you threaten legal action. They may be relevant to the overall context, but they should not be treated as proof of financial wrongdoing on their own.

I would not recommend sending the draft reply accusing him of theft or contacting customers and staff. That could escalate the dispute and may make it harder to obtain documents.

The next step is to send a controlled request for information, including board minutes, management accounts, supplier cost breakdowns, director expense details, bank statements for relevant periods, and any decision not to declare dividends. Once those documents are reviewed, the supervising solicitor can advise on the strength of any formal position.

Kind regards,
[Name]

## AI Skill 1 - Model Answer

A good student may use AI to propose a structure, but should revise it so that it matches the audience, the factual record, and the purpose of the communication. The student should check that the AI has not introduced new facts or legal conclusions.

## AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

A good student should identify and remove overstatement, blame language, premature threats, unsupported admissions, and vague drafting. AI suggestions should be treated as drafting options, not final judgment.

## AI Skill 3 - Model Answer

A good student should compare the AI fact/assumption list against all six exhibits. Confirmed facts should be separated from disputed points, inferred points, and missing evidence. Any hallucinated or unsupported AI output should be corrected.

## AI Section Completion Standard - Tutor Guidance

A complete answer shows that the student used AI to improve clarity and organisation while retaining human responsibility for accuracy, professional tone, fact checking, caveats, and final judgment.

# AI Simulation Agent Brief

Simulation Type: Clarification call before writing or live feedback on a written message.

Agent Role: Supervisor or client contact, depending on platform setup.

Agent Persona: A time-pressured stakeholder who wants a useful case update email and is concerned about minority shareholder dispute in a family-owned bakery business.

Simulation Purpose: The student practises clarifying audience, purpose, tone, missing information, and unsafe wording before finalising the written communication.

Opening Statement: Thanks for looking at this. I need a clear written response, but I do not want us to say anything that creates unnecessary risk. What do you think we can safely say?

## Core Facts the Agent Knows

A minority shareholder wants a clear update after receiving incomplete accounts and tense family messages. She wants a strong letter, but the evidence is incomplete and sensitive.

The final communication must be practical and evidence-led.

There are missing or disputed facts that should not be glossed over.

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character and do not give model answers.

Ask the student to justify wording by reference to the exhibits.

Push back if the student uses unsupported allegations or promises.

Reward clear, measured, audience-aware communication.

Do not invent new facts beyond the exhibits and brief.
