**LD-02 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS**

Scenario: Drafting a Remediation Notice for Commercial Cleaning Failures

Skill: Legal Drafting

Industry / Practice Area: Property Management / Facilities Services

# Task 1 — Model Answer

The drafting objective is to prepare a first working draft of the contractual remediation notice for Westvale Property Management. The draft should support draft a remediation notice requiring brightpath to correct cleaning service issues while preserving westvale’s contractual position and avoiding premature termination wording. It should be practical, evidence-based, and suitable for supervisor review rather than sending immediately. The purpose is not to make final legal findings, but to convert the client’s commercial objective into controlled wording that preserves position, records any conditions, and avoids unnecessary escalation.

# Task 2 — Model Answer

The draft should accurately identify Westvale Property Management, BrightPath Cleaning Ltd, the relevant client contact (Omar Linden, Facilities Manager), and the matter context. It should use the document titles and dates from the exhibits, including the instruction document, the relevant contract or policy extract, and the correspondence or operational records. Any defined terms should be consistent throughout. If authority to sign, approve, vary, or send the document is unclear, the draft should flag that as a supervisor approval point rather than assume authority.

# Task 3 — Model Answer

Facts that can safely be used include: Westvale manages a serviced office building with tenant complaints; BrightPath missed or partly missed several evening cleaning tasks; the contract requires a written remediation notice and a cure plan before termination for service failure. The draft should not present uncertain points as established facts. The following should be treated cautiously: whether all missed cleans were BrightPath’s fault; whether Westvale provided alternative access promptly; whether the service standards were measured correctly. The wording should say “our understanding is”, “the records indicate”, or “please confirm” where the evidence is incomplete. It should avoid phrases such as “you admit”, “you are solely responsible”, or “there is no dispute” unless the exhibits clearly support them.

# Task 4 — Model Answer

The drafting basis is the practical and contractual framework shown in the exhibits, not a jurisdiction-specific statute or legal test. The draft should rely on the relevant agreement, policy, instruction, or commercial process and should reflect any variation, notice, approval, cure, or authority requirements shown in the documents. Where the basis is not fully clear, the draft should preserve rights and state that the position is subject to review and supervisor confirmation.

# Task 5 — Model Answer

The intended outcome is to give Westvale Property Management a controlled written document that advances its objective while reducing drafting risk. The draft should include the key obligations, protections, conditions, reservations, or approvals required in this scenario. It should also preserve commercial flexibility where appropriate. The answer should recognise these risks: sending immediate termination contrary to process; ignoring access failures caused by the building system; using emotive tenant complaint language as established fact. It should not overreach by demanding outcomes that the evidence or instructions do not yet support.

# Task 6 — Model Answer

Information to clarify includes: whether all missed cleans were BrightPath’s fault; whether Westvale provided alternative access promptly; whether the service standards were measured correctly. The student should also check final commercial instructions, names and signing authority, exact dates and deadlines, whether any internal or counterparty approval is required, and whether the supervisor wants express reservation-of-rights wording. Any operational promise in the draft should be checked with the client before inclusion.

# Task 7 — Model Answer

Unsafe wording would include language that makes admissions, asserts blame without evidence, assumes legal consequences, or threatens action that the contract/process may not allow. In this matter, the student should avoid: sending immediate termination contrary to process; ignoring access failures caused by the building system; using emotive tenant complaint language as established fact. The safer approach is to use neutral, precise language: “the available records indicate”, “please provide”, “subject to our client’s rights”, “without admission”, “for review”, or “subject to approval”, depending on the draft.

# Task 8 — Model Answer

The document structure should start by identifying the parties, the purpose of the draft, and the relevant background. It should then set out the operative wording: obligations or commitments, dates, conditions, evidence or documents required, any reservation of rights, and next steps. The final section should make clear what is required from BrightPath Cleaning Ltd, what remains subject to approval, and who should respond. The order should make the document easy to use operationally.

# Task 9 — Model Answer

A strong draft would use concise operative wording. Example: “This draft is prepared on the basis of the information currently available and is subject to supervisor review. Our client’s position is reserved. The parties agree/are asked to confirm the following: [insert specific obligation], by [date], with supporting confirmation of [evidence], and without prejudice to any outstanding points identified in the correspondence.” The exact wording should be tailored to the document type and should not include unsupported admissions or threats.

# Task 10 — Model Answer

The final checklist should ask: have the parties and dates been checked; does the draft match the exhibits; are uncertain facts labelled as uncertain; is authority to send or sign confirmed; are all deadlines workable; are defined terms consistent; does the draft avoid jurisdiction-specific assumptions; are admissions and threats removed; are reservations and conditions included; and has the supervisor approved the final form before use.

# Main Practical Task — Full Model Answer

Draft Remediation Notice

Dear BrightPath,

We write on behalf of Westvale Property Management regarding cleaning services at the serviced office building. The tenant complaint log records concerns about uncollected waste, kitchen cleaning, and washroom supplies between 28 March and 10 April 2027. We note your response of 11 April, including your comments about access issues on 30 March and 4 April.

This notice is issued under the remediation process in clause 12 of the cleaning services agreement. Westvale requires BrightPath to provide, within five business days, a written remediation plan addressing: evening kitchen checks; waste removal; washroom supply checks; supervisor sign-off; and communication where access issues arise.

Westvale reserves its position regarding the causes of the service issues, including the access control incidents, and all rights under the agreement. Please do not treat this letter as immediate termination. The purpose is to secure prompt remediation and a reliable service plan before further steps are considered.

In this scenario, the key danger is that AI may ignore evidence gaps or assume that Westvale Property Management has a stronger position than the documents support. The safer tone depends on the client’s objective and evidence. A good answer explains why the chosen wording is proportionate, avoids blame, preserves rights, and remains usable after supervisor review.
