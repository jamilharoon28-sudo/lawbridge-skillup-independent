### Case Simulation 16 | NP-02

## Preparing to Settle a Supplier Delay Dispute

## Learning Outcomes

- Prepare settlement goals and alternatives.

- Assess evidence strengths and weaknesses before negotiating.

- Identify relationship and future supply risks.

- Use practical settlement options.

- Prepare a clear negotiation strategy table.

### Student Task Brief

A retailer missed a seasonal sales window after a supplier delivered packaging late. The supplier denies full responsibility and wants to preserve the relationship. The client wants compensation but also needs future supply.

### Student Practice Tasks

Complete the following practice tasks as a trainee-style matter file review. Your answers must cite the relevant exhibit, clause, date, email, log entry or policy wording. Do not give broad commentary where a specific document reference is available.

## Detailed Practice Tasks

1. Build a chronology of order, delay and sales impact.

2. Identify evidence supporting the client's loss.

3. Identify evidence that weakens the client's position.

4. Prepare a settlement range and non-money options.

5. Identify future supply and relationship concerns.

6. Draft a measured opening statement.

7. Prepare supplier questions.

8. Identify what needs client authority before the call.

9. Prepare a next-step email after the negotiation.

10. Reflect on when settlement may be better than escalation.

## Working Method

- Start with the exhibit index, but do not treat it as a summary of the answer.

- Mark every clause, date, figure and disputed factual point that affects advice or drafting.

- Separate confirmed facts, disputed facts, assumptions, missing information and legal points requiring further checking.

- Keep supervisor-facing outputs concise: lead with the practical risk, then the evidence, then the next step.
