**Tutor Use Note**

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibit documents.

The model answers indicate the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

**Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.**

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

**Matter Snapshot for Tutors**

A business wants to use AI tools to create marketing materials, reports and software-related content. You must draft contract wording that allocates risk and responsibility for AI-assisted outputs.

**Required Output Guidance**

Draft AI clause

Definitions, ownership/use rights, human review, third-party claims, confidentiality and record-keeping.

Evidence standard: Must be practical and internally consistent.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

**Drafting note**

Rationale, assumptions, uncertainty and fallback options.

Evidence standard: Must avoid overstating legal position.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Risk allocation table

Risks allocated to client, supplier or shared process.

Evidence standard: Each risk must match the clause wording.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.
