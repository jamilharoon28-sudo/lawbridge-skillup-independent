### Tutor Use Note

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibits.

The model answers show the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

## Required Output Guidance

Primary practical output

Email, plan, note, table, script, checklist or update required by the scenario.

Evidence standard: Must be clear, realistic and suitable for the intended reader.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Risk or issue list

What might go wrong, why it matters, who is affected and what should happen next.

Evidence standard: Must separate service, communication, professional, factual and escalation risks.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Reflection or improvement note

What the student learned, what they would do differently and what professional judgment was required.

Evidence standard: Must be honest, practical and grounded in the file.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

> AI Extension Model Answers - Three Scenario Questions

> Tutor assessment focus Tutors should assess only three AI questions. Strong answers must be grounded in PS-COM-01 - Explaining a Complex Issue to a Non-Lawyer Founder and the matching exhibit bundle, not generic AI risk language.

> - AI Task 1 - Human Skill First Strong answer identifies three priority issues from the exhibit bundle, explains why they matter for Communication Skills, separates confirmed facts from gaps, and states what must be checked or escalated before AI is used.

> - AI Task 2 - AI Tool Usage and Critique Strong answer gives one safe prompt limited to PS-COM-01 - Explaining a Complex Issue to a Non-Lawyer Founder; asks for structure, critique or drafting support only; requires primary practical output, risk or issue list, reflection or improvement note; prohibits invented facts or assumed law; and explains how the output will be checked against exhibits and source reliability.

> - AI Task 3 - Judgment and AI Supervision Under Pressure Strong answer explains what the student would accept, change or reject from AI output; keeps caveats visible; identifies missing evidence and approval points; and produces a supervisor-ready position grounded in the scenario.

| Common weaknesses to mark down |
| --- |
| Generic AI commentary; asking AI for the final answer; failure to cite or check exhibits; hiding uncertainty; no supervisor escalation where facts, law or approvals remain unresolved. |
