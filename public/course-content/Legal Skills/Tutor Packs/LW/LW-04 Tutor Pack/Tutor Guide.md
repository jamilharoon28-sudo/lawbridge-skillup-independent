# LW-04 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS

## Task 1 - Model Answer

The audience is the contractor opponent. The purpose is to produce a opponent letter that helps the reader understand the position, practical risk, and next step. The response should be firm, factual, proportionate, and non-inflammatory and should not go beyond the evidence.

## Task 2 - Model Answer

Key facts include: Milo & Finch says Stonebar Fit-Out missed the agreed completion date and left the cafe unable to open for two weeks; The fit-out began on 3 February; The contract requires completion by the agreed date subject to written variations, client access, and agreed changes. The facts should be presented in a logical order and tied back to the main written response rather than listed without analysis.

## Task 3 - Model Answer

The issues are factual uncertainty, communication risk, evidence gaps, and the need to write in a way that is useful without overstating the legal position. The student should identify both the supported concerns and the limits of the evidence.

## Task 4 - Model Answer

The client objective is to send a professional opponent letter preserving the client position while inviting a practical resolution. The written response should advance that objective while preserving credibility, avoiding unnecessary escalation, and leaving room for supervisor review.

## Task 5 - Model Answer

The communication must explain the supported facts, the practical significance of those facts, the points that remain uncertain, and the recommended next steps. It should be specific enough to be useful and simple enough for the intended reader.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The student should avoid unsupported allegations, definitive conclusions not proved by the exhibits, unnecessary admissions, threats that have not been approved, and language that may damage credibility. In this scenario, avoid: calling the contractor dishonest or negligent without evidence; claiming all lost profits are proven; ignoring client-requested design changes; threatening action without supervisor approval.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Important caveats include the lack of complete evidence, unresolved factual disputes, and the need for supervisor review before making stronger assertions. The communication should distinguish known facts from allegations, assumptions, and points requiring follow-up.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible structure is: short introduction, confirmed factual summary, key issue or risk, caveats/missing evidence, practical recommendation, and next steps. The tone should be firm, factual, proportionate, and non-inflammatory.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Key points should include: the agreed works and revised completion dates; confirmed defects and delays; client losses as asserted, not finally verified; requested next steps and evidence needed. These should be drafted in complete, audience-appropriate sentences rather than as internal notes.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final communication should be accurate, concise, and usable. It should address the reader directly, include only supported facts, make uncertainty clear, and end with an actionable next step.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Dear Sirs,

We act for Milo & Finch Cafe Ltd in relation to the fit-out works at its new cafe premises.

Our client is concerned about the delay in practical handover and the unresolved snagging items following the works. The documents we have reviewed indicate that the original target completion date was 28 March, that a revised date of 8 April was discussed after changes to the counter layout and lighting, and that handover occurred on 17 April subject to snagging. The current snagging list includes uneven floor edging, incomplete shelf lighting, a leaking sink trap, and missing sealant behind the counter.

We note your email referring to the 12 March design change and the alleged missed access date on 2 April. Our client does not accept that those matters explain all delay or unresolved items, but we agree that they should be considered when narrowing the issues.

Please confirm by 4 pm on [date] when you will attend to inspect and remedy the outstanding snagging items. Please also provide your position on the completion delay, including any documents you rely on in relation to design variations, access, and revised completion dates.

Our client reserves its position in relation to any losses arising from delayed opening, including revenue impact, but those losses will need to be evidenced and assessed.

Yours faithfully,
[Name]

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character and do not give model answers.

Ask the student to justify wording by reference to the exhibits.

Push back if the student uses unsupported allegations or promises.

Reward clear, measured, audience-aware communication.

Do not invent new facts beyond the exhibits and brief.
