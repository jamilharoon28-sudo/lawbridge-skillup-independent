# Detailed Practice Tasks

## Task 1 - Identify audience and purpose

Identify who will read the client advice email, what the client advice email must achieve, and what the reader needs from it.

## Task 2 - Extract the key facts

Identify the key facts from the exhibits that should be used in the client advice email.

## Task 3 - Identify the legal and practical issues

Identify the legal, commercial, practical, professional, or communication issues that must be addressed.

## Task 4 - Identify the client objective

Identify the client objective and any commercial or personal priorities shaping the written response.

## Task 5 - Decide what must be explained

Decide what the final communication must explain clearly to be useful and complete.

## Task 6 - Decide what should not be said

Identify wording, conclusions, allegations, promises, or admissions that should be avoided at this stage.

## Task 7 - Identify uncertainty and caveats

Identify the uncertain facts, missing documents, and caveats that need to be reflected in the client advice email.

## Task 8 - Plan structure and tone

Plan the structure, tone, headings, and ordering of the final communication.

## Task 9 - Draft key points

Draft the key points or paragraphs that will form the core of the final communication.

## Task 10 - Prepare the final communication

Prepare the final professional written communication using the available evidence and appropriate caveats.
