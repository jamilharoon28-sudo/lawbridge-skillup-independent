# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Research note - Researching Restrictive Wording in a Freelancer Design Agreement

Research question: What points should be researched before advising whether broad post-project restrictions in a freelancer agreement are enforceable or commercially reasonable?

Short answer: The clause raises a real issue but the broad twelve-month wording may be open to challenge on reasonableness; a practical research note should separate confidentiality from the wider work restriction and recommend a consent-based next step.

Key facts: Leo signed a short freelancer agreement containing confidentiality and non-solicitation wording.; A schedule says Leo must not work with “any competing brand” for twelve months without written consent.; The project involved unreleased product packaging and launch strategy.; Leo has been offered work by a different brand selling unrelated haircare products..

Relevant principles: Post-engagement restrictions are usually assessed by scope, duration, legitimate interest, and practical reasonableness.; Confidentiality obligations are generally different from broad bans on future work.; A narrow consent request or undertaking about confidential information may be a practical alternative to a blanket dispute..

Application: On the current documents, Leo Marin has a credible practical point, but the strength of the position depends on the missing facts. The research should not present the client as certain to succeed, and it should not rely on jurisdiction-specific rules because no jurisdiction has been provided. The safer approach is to frame the position around supported facts, preserve evidence, request clarification, and seek a proportionate practical remedy or next step.

Uncertainties and further research: whether the restriction was clearly incorporated; whether the haircare brand is genuinely competing; what confidential information Leo still holds; whether the agency has applied the clause consistently.

Recommended next steps: ask the supervisor to confirm whether jurisdiction-specific research is required; request the missing documents; prepare a cautious factual request or response; and avoid final advice or external allegations until the evidence gaps have been reviewed.

## AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

A good student should compare the AI summary against the source extracts line by line. If AI turns a general principle into a hard rule, omits caveats, or invents a legal authority, the student should correct it and explain the uncertainty in the research note.

A good student should identify hallucinated citations, unsupported legal tests, overconfident conclusions, and invented facts. AI may assist with drafting, but it cannot be treated as a source of law or as a substitute for checking the provided source material.
