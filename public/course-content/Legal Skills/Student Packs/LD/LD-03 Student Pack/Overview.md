*Jurisdiction-neutral practical drafting simulation*

**Scenario Title** Drafting Ownership and Confidentiality Terms for a Design Settlement

**Industry / Practice Area** Creative Services / Branding / Freelance Design

**Client** MiraVale Skincare Ltd

**Counterparty / Other Party** Lena Frost Design Studio

**Core Drafting Output** settlement heads of terms clauses on design ownership and confidentiality

# Scenario Background:

MiraVale Skincare Ltd commissioned packaging and social media assets from a freelance designer. The relationship broke down when the designer posted draft packaging concepts online and MiraVale withheld part of the final invoice. Priya Raman wants all design assets transferred and wants the designer banned from mentioning the work. The documents show some ambiguity about ownership and portfolio rights. The drafting task is to prepare balanced settlement wording that secures practical rights without overreaching.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal professional assisting Daniel Osei, supervising solicitor. You have been asked to prepare the first working draft of the settlement heads of terms clauses on design ownership and confidentiality. You are not expected to give final legal advice or send the document. Your role is to organise the facts, identify drafting risks, prepare clear wording, and highlight points requiring supervisor approval.

# Core Skill Task:

Use the documents to prepare a clear, evidence-based, proportionate legal draft that reflects the client’s instructions while avoiding unsupported admissions, overstatement, ambiguity, and jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

# Main Practical Task:

Draft settlement clauses dealing with design asset ownership, limited portfolio use, confidentiality, and release of unpaid fees, subject to supervisor approval.
