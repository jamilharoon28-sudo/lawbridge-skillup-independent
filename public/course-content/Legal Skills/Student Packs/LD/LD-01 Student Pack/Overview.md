*Jurisdiction-neutral practical drafting simulation*

**Scenario Title** Drafting a Service Level Amendment for a Catering Supplier Agreement

**Industry / Practice Area** Hospitality / Event Catering / Commercial Contracts

**Client** Harbour House Events Ltd

**Counterparty / Other Party** Mason & Reed Catering Services

**Core Drafting Output** service level amendment letter and replacement service-level clause

# Scenario Background:

Harbour House Events Ltd operates three event venues. It relies on Mason & Reed Catering Services to provide catering teams and menus for corporate events. Recent staffing confirmations have arrived late, several menu substitutions have been made without written approval, and one corporate client has threatened to cancel a repeat booking. Nadia Ellis wants wording that tightens service levels before the summer event season. The documents show performance concerns but also show that Harbour House sometimes changed guest numbers late. The drafting task is to produce proportionate amendment wording that protects the client while leaving room for the commercial relationship to continue.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal professional assisting Ravi Shah, supervising solicitor. You have been asked to prepare the first working draft of the service level amendment letter and replacement service-level clause. You are not expected to give final legal advice or send the document. Your role is to organise the facts, identify drafting risks, prepare clear wording, and highlight points requiring supervisor approval.

# Documents / Information Available:

1. Exhibit 1 — Client instruction email from Nadia Ellis
2. Exhibit 2 — Current supplier agreement extract
3. Exhibit 3 — Event issue chronology
4. Exhibit 4 — Email chain about guest number changes
5. Exhibit 5 — Internal revenue note
6. Exhibit 6 — Flawed draft amendment prepared by the client

# Core Skill Task:

Use the documents to prepare a clear, evidence-based, proportionate legal draft that reflects the client’s instructions while avoiding unsupported admissions, overstatement, ambiguity, and jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

# Main Practical Task:

Draft a short service level amendment and covering note for supervisor review, addressing late staffing confirmations and delivery quality issues without overstating breach.
