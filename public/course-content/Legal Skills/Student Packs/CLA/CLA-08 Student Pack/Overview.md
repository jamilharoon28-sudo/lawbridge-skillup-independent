# Scenario Title:

Assessing a Renewable Energy Offtake Proposal for a Manufacturing Client

# Industry / Practice Area:

Renewable energy / manufacturing / long-term supply

# Scenario Background:

Keystone Components Ltd is considering whether to sign a 7-year renewable energy offtake arrangement. The potential value or exposure is approximately USD 5.6 million projected total spend. The commercial team wants a practical answer by the energy procurement decision meeting next Tuesday. The business sees opportunity, but the materials show legal, operational, financial, and communication risks. The key tension is that the projected savings depend on price assumptions and minimum volume commitments that may not match keystone demand The learner must prepare commercial legal analysis that helps the business decide what to do next without overstating the evidence or ignoring commercial priorities.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal team member assisting the supervising solicitor. You have been asked to review the documents, identify the legal and commercial risks, prepare a business-facing analysis, and support a discussion with the business stakeholder. You are not expected to make the final business decision.

# Core Skill Task:

Analyse a commercial problem through a legal lens and translate the risks into practical business options, safeguards, and next steps.

# Main Practical Task:

Prepare a commercial legal risk note for Aisha Nouri, explaining the key risks, options, safeguards, missing evidence, and recommended next steps on long-term renewable energy offtake proposal. Use the note to support a business briefing conversation.
