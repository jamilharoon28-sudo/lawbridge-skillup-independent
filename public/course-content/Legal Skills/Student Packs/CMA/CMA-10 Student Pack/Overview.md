*Analysing a Logistics Damage and Lost Stock Claim*

**Scenario Title** Analysing a Logistics Damage and Lost Stock Claim

**Industry / Practice Area** Logistics / Supply Chain / Wholesale Goods

**Difficulty** Intermediate

**Scenario Type** Case and matter analysis simulation

# Scenario Background

Marston Cycle Components Ltd has asked for help with damaged and missing bike component shipment following a warehouse transfer. Ibrahim Noor, Finance Director, wants the legal team to help prepare a case analysis identifying liability, evidence gaps and settlement options. The client has a major customer waiting and wants to claim the full retail value immediately. The available file is incomplete and contains conflicting accounts, so the student must analyse the matter carefully before any external communication or recommendation is made.

Central tension: The client says the goods were intact at collection; the carrier says packaging was already crushed and the delivery note was signed clean. The matter cannot be resolved by accepting either side's account without further evidence.

# Student Role

You are acting as a junior legal professional in the team. You report to Clara Evans. You are not expected to make final strategic decisions or send external correspondence. Your task is to analyse the matter file, organise the evidence, identify strengths and weaknesses, and prepare a clear note for supervisor review.

# Documents / Information Available

1. Exhibit 6 - Supervisor Instruction and Risk Note

# Core Skill Task

Analyse a realistic matter file, identify the key issues and evidence gaps, and prepare a practical case/matter analysis note for a supervising solicitor or matter lead.

# Main Practical Task

Prepare a concise case/matter analysis note for Clara Evans. Your note should summarise the client objective, key facts, issue list, strengths, weaknesses, evidence gaps, communication risks, recommended next steps, and decisions requiring supervisor input.
