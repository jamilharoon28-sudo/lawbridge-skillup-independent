# Detailed Practice Tasks

## Task 1 — Identify the drafting objective

Identify the document you have been asked to draft and explain its practical purpose in this matter.

## Task 2 — Identify the parties and key details

Identify the parties, dates, document references, decision-makers, and any defined terms that the draft will need to use accurately.

## Task 3 — Identify the factual basis

Identify the facts from the exhibits that can safely be used in the draft, separating confirmed facts from allegations, assumptions, or unclear points.

## Task 4 — Identify the legal, commercial, or contractual basis

Identify the contractual, procedural, commercial, or professional basis for the draft, without making jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

## Task 5 — Identify the intended outcome and protections

Identify the outcome the client wants and the obligations, protections, concessions, reservations, or safeguards that should be reflected in the draft.

## Task 6 — Identify missing information

Identify the information, documents, dates, authority, or supervisor decisions that must be clarified before the draft is finalised or sent.

## Task 7 — Identify risky wording to avoid

Identify any wording that would be unsafe, too aggressive, too vague, unsupported by the evidence, or likely to weaken the client’s position.

## Task 8 — Plan the document structure

Plan the structure of the draft, including the order of sections, clauses, paragraphs, or key messages.

## Task 9 — Draft the key wording

Draft the key clauses, paragraphs, or operative wording that should appear in the document.

## Task 10 — Prepare a final drafting checklist

Prepare a final checklist of points to verify before the draft is approved or sent.
