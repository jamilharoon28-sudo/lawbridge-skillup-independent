# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Research note - Researching Takedown Options for Copied Product Images

Research question: What practical takedown or complaint options should be researched where product images and descriptions appear copied?

Short answer: The client likely has a practical basis to prepare a platform complaint, but ownership evidence and original image files should be gathered before sending a formal takedown demand.

Key facts: Olive & Reed commissioned product photographs from a freelancer.; The competitor listing uses similar angles, props, and text but has cropped watermarks removed.; The freelancer agreement is unsigned in the copy provided.; Platform rules allow complaints for original content but require proof of rights ownership..

Relevant principles: A content complaint is stronger where the complainant can show ownership, licence, or authority to act.; Similarity alone may not prove copying; metadata, original files, publication dates, and photographer rights matter.; Platform takedown processes often require precise URLs, original works, and a rights statement..

Application: On the current documents, Olive & Reed Homeware has a credible practical point, but the strength of the position depends on the missing facts. The research should not present the client as certain to succeed, and it should not rely on jurisdiction-specific rules because no jurisdiction has been provided. The safer approach is to frame the position around supported facts, preserve evidence, request clarification, and seek a proportionate practical remedy or next step.

Uncertainties and further research: whether the retailer owns copyright or only has a licence; whether the competitor used the exact images or recreated them; whether watermarks were removed by the competitor; whether the freelancer can provide original files.

Recommended next steps: ask the supervisor to confirm whether jurisdiction-specific research is required; request the missing documents; prepare a cautious factual request or response; and avoid final advice or external allegations until the evidence gaps have been reviewed.
