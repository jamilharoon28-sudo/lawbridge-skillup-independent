**Interviewing an Anxious Tenant About Urgent Repairs and a Threatened Move-Out**

**Scenario Title**

Interviewing an Anxious Tenant About Urgent Repairs and a Threatened Move-Out

**Industry / Practice Area**

Residential property / consumer dispute / landlord and tenant administration

## Scenario Background

Maya Rahman rents a small flat above a shop. She says water has been entering the bedroom wall for weeks and that mould has affected her child’s asthma. The letting agent says repairs were delayed because Maya missed access appointments. Maya wants to leave immediately, recover temporary accommodation costs, and send a firm message tonight. The documents are incomplete and some dates do not align. The student must prepare for a careful client interview without giving premature legal advice or assuming the landlord is solely responsible.

## Student Role

You are acting as a trainee solicitor or junior legal professional preparing for an initial client interview. Your task is to organise the information, prepare a structured interview, avoid premature advice, and support the supervising solicitor after the interview.

## Core Skill Task

Prepare for and conduct a realistic client interview, then produce a short post-interview summary for the supervising solicitor.

## Main Practical Task

Conduct a structured client interview and produce a short post-interview summary for the supervising solicitor. Your final output should identify what is known, what remains uncertain, what documents are needed, what the client wants, and what should be escalated before any advice or external communication is given.
