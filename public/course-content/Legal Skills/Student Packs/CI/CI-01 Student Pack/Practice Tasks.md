# Detailed Practice Tasks

## Task 1 — Identify the client, matter, and purpose of the interview.

Identify who the client is, who the other party is, what the matter concerns, why the interview is needed, and what the interview must achieve before any advice is given.

## Task 2 — Identify what is already known from the documents.

Using the exhibits, summarise the facts that appear to be known at this stage, including key dates, payments, documents, alleged defects, messages, and the current dispute.

## Task 3 — Identify what is missing or unclear.

List the main facts, documents, and explanations that remain missing or uncertain before the supervising solicitor can safely advise Ms Patel.

## Task 4 — Prepare open questions for the client.

Prepare open, non-leading questions that help Ms Patel explain what happened in her own words, including her objectives, timeline, conversations, and concerns.

## Task 5 — Prepare focused follow-up questions.

Prepare focused follow-up questions about the quote, agreed scope, pantry variation, completion sign-off, defects, invoice, evidence, and any communication with BrightBuild.

## Task 6 — Identify sensitive issues and how to ask about them.

Identify the sensitive or potentially difficult issues in the interview and explain how you would ask about them without blaming the client or giving premature advice.

## Task 7 — Identify legal and professional boundaries for the interview.

Explain what you should and should not say during the interview, including limits on legal advice, payment decisions, public posts, confidentiality, and supervisor review.

## Task 8 — Plan how to explain confidentiality, role, and next steps.

Prepare a short client-friendly opening explanation covering your role, the purpose of the interview, confidentiality, note-taking, supervisor review, and next steps.

## Task 9 — Plan how to handle difficult emotions or inconsistent answers.

Explain how you would respond if Ms Patel is upset, angry, uncertain, or gives information that conflicts with the documents.

## Task 10 — Prepare an interview structure.

Create a practical interview structure from opening to closing, including agenda, fact gathering, document review, follow-up questions, summary, and next steps.
