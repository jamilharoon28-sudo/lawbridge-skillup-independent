**Tutor Use Note**

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibit documents.

The model answers indicate the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

**Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.**

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

**Matter Snapshot for Tutors**

A payment services client has received a serious complaint from a major merchant after a service failure. You must draft a professional response and internal writing note that manages liability, evidence and customer relationship risk.

**Required Output Guidance**

Complaint response letter/email

Acknowledgement, factual position, investigation status, next steps and relationship management.

Evidence standard: Must avoid unsupported admissions.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Internal writing note

Risky phrases to avoid, evidence gaps and approval steps.

Evidence standard: Must cite file evidence.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Follow-up action list

Technical, commercial and legal steps before final settlement or compensation.

Evidence standard: Each action must have an owner.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.
