**Interviewing an Employee Facing Suspension After a Workplace Incident**

**Scenario Title**

Interviewing an Employee Facing Suspension After a Workplace Incident

**Industry / Practice Area**

Employment / workplace investigation / professional conduct and procedural risk

## Scenario Background

Noah Miles, a warehouse supervisor, has been suspended pending investigation after a heated exchange with a temporary worker and an alleged failure to follow safety procedures. Noah says the allegation is exaggerated and that he was under-staffed. Internal messages suggest there may have been earlier warnings, an incomplete incident report, and a possible witness who was not named in the employer’s letter. The student must prepare for an interview that gathers a balanced account and avoids turning Noah’s assertions into established facts.

## Student Role

You are acting as a trainee solicitor or junior legal professional preparing for an initial client interview. Your task is to organise the information, prepare a structured interview, avoid premature advice, and support the supervising solicitor after the interview.

## Main Practical Task

Conduct a structured client interview and produce a short post-interview summary for the supervising solicitor. Your final output should identify what is known, what remains uncertain, what documents are needed, what the client wants, and what should be escalated before any advice or external communication is given.
