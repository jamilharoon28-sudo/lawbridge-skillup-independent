*Analysing a Customer Data Incident File*

**Scenario Title** Analysing a Customer Data Incident File

**Industry / Practice Area** E-Commerce / Data Handling / Retail Operations

**Difficulty** Intermediate

**Scenario Type** Case and matter analysis simulation

# Scenario Background

BrightCart Online Ltd has asked for help with accidental exposure of customer order data through a support portal export. Sofia Mendes, Head of Customer Operations, wants the legal team to help identify facts, risks, evidence gaps, and immediate action points for a senior review note. A customer has posted screenshots online and the business wants to issue a public reassurance today. The available file is incomplete and contains conflicting accounts, so the student must analyse the matter carefully before any external communication or recommendation is made.

Central tension: Internal IT says only test data was exported, but customer screenshots suggest real names and addresses appeared. The matter cannot be resolved by accepting either side's account without further evidence.

# Student Role

You are acting as a junior legal professional in the team. You report to Jonah Reed. You are not expected to make final strategic decisions or send external correspondence. Your task is to analyse the matter file, organise the evidence, identify strengths and weaknesses, and prepare a clear note for supervisor review.

# Documents / Information Available

1. Exhibit 1 - File Opening Note and Client Objective
1. Exhibit 2 - Chronology of Key Events
1. Exhibit 3 - Key Correspondence Extract
1. Exhibit 4 - Relevant Agreement or Policy Extract
1. Exhibit 5 - Conflicting Evidence Summary
1. Exhibit 6 - Supervisor Instruction and Risk Note

# Core Skill Task

Analyse a realistic matter file, identify the key issues and evidence gaps, and prepare a practical case/matter analysis note for a supervising solicitor or matter lead.

# Main Practical Task

Prepare a concise case/matter analysis note for Jonah Reed. Your note should summarise the client objective, key facts, issue list, strengths, weaknesses, evidence gaps, communication risks, recommended next steps, and decisions requiring supervisor input.
