*Analysing a Subcontractor Payment and Defects Dispute*

**Scenario Title** Analysing a Subcontractor Payment and Defects Dispute

**Industry / Practice Area** Construction / Fit-Out / Commercial Projects

**Difficulty** Intermediate

**Scenario Type** Case and matter analysis simulation

# Scenario Background

Limeway Interiors Ltd has asked for help with unpaid fit-out invoices and alleged defects on a boutique hotel refurbishment. Daniel Okoro, Operations Director, wants the legal team to help decide whether to pursue payment, propose remedial works, or prepare a negotiated settlement position. Cash flow is tight and the client wants to threaten suspension on another project with the same developer. The available file is incomplete and contains conflicting accounts, so the student must analyse the matter carefully before any external communication or recommendation is made.

Central tension: Limeway says practical completion was achieved; Ravenstone says defects and missing snagging records justify withholding payment. The matter cannot be resolved by accepting either side's account without further evidence.

# Student Role

You are acting as a junior legal professional in the team. You report to Amelia Hart. You are not expected to make final strategic decisions or send external correspondence. Your task is to analyse the matter file, organise the evidence, identify strengths and weaknesses, and prepare a clear note for supervisor review.

Prepare a concise case/matter analysis note for Amelia Hart. Your note should summarise the client objective, key facts, issue list, strengths, weaknesses, evidence gaps, communication risks, recommended next steps, and decisions requiring supervisor input.
