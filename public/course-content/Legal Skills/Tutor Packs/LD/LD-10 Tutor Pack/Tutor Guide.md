**LD-10 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS**

Scenario: Drafting a Private Clinic Complaint Response and Records Request Letter

Skill: Legal Drafting

Industry / Practice Area: Healthcare Services / Private Clinic / Complaint Handling

# Task 1 — Model Answer

The drafting objective is to prepare a first working draft of the patient complaint response and records request acknowledgement for Riverton Wellness Clinic. The draft should support draft a careful patient complaint response acknowledging concerns, requesting clarification, preserving confidentiality, and setting out next practical steps without making unsupported admissions. It should be practical, evidence-based, and suitable for supervisor review rather than sending immediately. The purpose is not to make final legal findings, but to convert the client’s commercial objective into controlled wording that preserves position, records any conditions, and avoids unnecessary escalation.

# Task 2 — Model Answer

The draft should accurately identify Riverton Wellness Clinic, Patient: Clara Jain, the relevant client contact (Dr. Marcus Leigh, Clinic Director), and the matter context. It should use the document titles and dates from the exhibits, including the instruction document, the relevant contract or policy extract, and the correspondence or operational records. Any defined terms should be consistent throughout. If authority to sign, approve, vary, or send the document is unclear, the draft should flag that as a supervisor approval point rather than assume authority.

# Task 3 — Model Answer

Facts that can safely be used include: Clara Jain complained about a cancelled appointment and possible confidentiality issue; appointment system shows cancellation but not full reason; staff accounts differ on what was said at reception. The draft should not present uncertain points as established facts. The following should be treated cautiously: what exactly was said in reception; who was nearby at the time; why the cancellation reason was not fully recorded. The wording should say “our understanding is”, “the records indicate”, or “please confirm” where the evidence is incomplete. It should avoid phrases such as “you admit”, “you are solely responsible”, or “there is no dispute” unless the exhibits clearly support them.

# Task 4 — Model Answer

The drafting basis is the practical and contractual framework shown in the exhibits, not a jurisdiction-specific statute or legal test. The draft should rely on the relevant agreement, policy, instruction, or commercial process and should reflect any variation, notice, approval, cure, or authority requirements shown in the documents. Where the basis is not fully clear, the draft should preserve rights and state that the position is subject to review and supervisor confirmation.

# Task 5 — Model Answer

The intended outcome is to give Riverton Wellness Clinic a controlled written document that advances its objective while reducing drafting risk. The draft should include the key obligations, protections, conditions, reservations, or approvals required in this scenario. It should also preserve commercial flexibility where appropriate. The answer should recognise these risks: admitting breach before review; using defensive or dismissive wording; failing to handle records request separately and confidentially. It should not overreach by demanding outcomes that the evidence or instructions do not yet support.

# Task 6 — Model Answer

Information to clarify includes: what exactly was said in reception; who was nearby at the time; why the cancellation reason was not fully recorded. The student should also check final commercial instructions, names and signing authority, exact dates and deadlines, whether any internal or counterparty approval is required, and whether the supervisor wants express reservation-of-rights wording. Any operational promise in the draft should be checked with the client before inclusion.

# Task 7 — Model Answer

Unsafe wording would include language that makes admissions, asserts blame without evidence, assumes legal consequences, or threatens action that the contract/process may not allow. In this matter, the student should avoid: admitting breach before review; using defensive or dismissive wording; failing to handle records request separately and confidentially. The safer approach is to use neutral, precise language: “the available records indicate”, “please provide”, “subject to our client’s rights”, “without admission”, “for review”, or “subject to approval”, depending on the draft.

# Task 8 — Model Answer

The document structure should start by identifying the parties, the purpose of the draft, and the relevant background. It should then set out the operative wording: obligations or commitments, dates, conditions, evidence or documents required, any reservation of rights, and next steps. The final section should make clear what is required from Patient: Clara Jain, what remains subject to approval, and who should respond. The order should make the document easy to use operationally.

# Task 9 — Model Answer

A strong draft would use concise operative wording. Example: “This draft is prepared on the basis of the information currently available and is subject to supervisor review. Our client’s position is reserved. The parties agree/are asked to confirm the following: [insert specific obligation], by [date], with supporting confirmation of [evidence], and without prejudice to any outstanding points identified in the correspondence.” The exact wording should be tailored to the document type and should not include unsupported admissions or threats.

# Task 10 — Model Answer

The final checklist should ask: have the parties and dates been checked; does the draft match the exhibits; are uncertain facts labelled as uncertain; is authority to send or sign confirmed; are all deadlines workable; are defined terms consistent; does the draft avoid jurisdiction-specific assumptions; are admissions and threats removed; are reservations and conditions included; and has the supervisor approved the final form before use.

# Main Practical Task — Full Model Answer

Draft Complaint Response

Dear Ms Jain,

Thank you for contacting Riverton Wellness Clinic. We are sorry that you experienced distress in connection with your appointment on 29 September and that you are concerned about how information was discussed at reception.

We are reviewing the appointment record, staff notes, and the circumstances of the cancellation. We have not yet reached final conclusions and will respond further once that review is complete. It would help us if you could confirm who was present, where you were standing, and what words you recall being used.

Your request for records will be handled through our secure records process. We will confirm the next practical steps and any identity or delivery requirements separately.

We aim to provide a substantive update within the timeframe set out in our complaints process. Thank you for raising these concerns with us.

# AI Skill 1 — Model Answer

AI may be used to suggest a structure for the patient complaint response and records request acknowledgement, but the student must check the structure against the exhibits. A good response would identify where AI cannot decide final wording: factual uncertainty, contractual procedure, authority to sign or send, commercial concessions, and supervisor approval. AI output should be treated as a drafting aid, not a source of facts or legal authority.

# AI Skill 2 — Model Answer

A good AI risk review would flag overstatement, admissions, threats, vague obligations, undefined terms, and missing dates. The student should then test the AI review against the actual exhibits. In this scenario, the key danger is that AI may ignore evidence gaps or assume that Riverton Wellness Clinic has a stronger position than the documents support. The student should remove invented facts and jurisdiction-specific assertions.

# AI Skill 3 — Model Answer

The safer tone depends on the client’s objective and evidence. A firm tone may be appropriate for clear obligations and deadlines, while a collaborative tone may preserve the relationship where the evidence is mixed. A good answer explains why the chosen wording is proportionate, avoids blame, preserves rights, and remains usable after supervisor review.

# AI Section Completion Standard — Tutor Guidance

A complete answer shows safe AI use: the student uses AI to organise or improve drafting, checks all output against the exhibits, identifies assumptions and missing information, avoids invented legal rules or facts, and retains human professional judgment. The answer should be practical, scenario-specific, and suitable for supervisor review.
