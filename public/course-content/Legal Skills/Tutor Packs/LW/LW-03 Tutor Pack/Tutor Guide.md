# LW-03 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS

## Task 1 - Model Answer

The audience is the patient complainant. The purpose is to produce a complaint response letter that helps the reader understand the position, practical risk, and next step. The response should be empathetic, accountable where facts are confirmed, clear, and careful and should not go beyond the evidence.

## Task 2 - Model Answer

Key facts include: Patient Lena Ortiz says her mole-check appointment was cancelled 40 minutes before it was due to start; The system shows an appointment booked for 11 July at 15:30; An email sent at 15:12 went to an address ending “mailbox. The facts should be presented in a logical order and tied back to the main written response rather than listed without analysis.

## Task 3 - Model Answer

The issues are factual uncertainty, communication risk, evidence gaps, and the need to write in a way that is useful without overstating the legal position. The student should identify both the supported concerns and the limits of the evidence.

## Task 4 - Model Answer

The client objective is to respond to the patient complaint without admitting unverified facts or minimising concern. The written response should advance that objective while preserving credibility, avoiding unnecessary escalation, and leaving room for supervisor review.

## Task 5 - Model Answer

The communication must explain the supported facts, the practical significance of those facts, the points that remain uncertain, and the recommended next steps. It should be specific enough to be useful and simple enough for the intended reader.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The student should avoid unsupported allegations, definitive conclusions not proved by the exhibits, unnecessary admissions, threats that have not been approved, and language that may damage credibility. In this scenario, avoid: calling the incident a confirmed breach before assessment; blaming the patient for contact details without evidence; promising compensation without authority; using defensive or dismissive wording.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Important caveats include the lack of complete evidence, unresolved factual disputes, and the need for supervisor review before making stronger assertions. The communication should distinguish known facts from allegations, assumptions, and points requiring follow-up.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible structure is: short introduction, confirmed factual summary, key issue or risk, caveats/missing evidence, practical recommendation, and next steps. The tone should be empathetic, accountable where facts are confirmed, clear, and careful.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Key points should include: what has been confirmed about the appointment cancellation; what is being checked about the email address error; what practical steps the clinic will take; how the clinic will communicate next steps. These should be drafted in complete, audience-appropriate sentences rather than as internal notes.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final communication should be accurate, concise, and usable. It should address the reader directly, include only supported facts, make uncertainty clear, and end with an actionable next step.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Dear Ms Ortiz,

Thank you for raising your concerns about the cancelled appointment on 11 July and the follow-up email sent afterwards. I am sorry for the disruption and concern this has caused.

We have completed an initial check of the appointment record. The system shows that your 15:30 appointment was cancelled after the clinician became unavailable that afternoon. A voicemail was left at 14:45, but we cannot assume from the records that you received it before travelling or preparing for the appointment. We should have communicated the cancellation as clearly and promptly as possible.

We are also checking the email issue. Our records show that a rebooking email was sent to an older email address, despite a newer address also being held on the system. The email appears to have included your first name, the appointment date, and a request to rebook. It does not appear from the initial audit to have included clinical photographs or a diagnosis. We are still checking whether any further information was included and what containment steps are available.

We will now escalate this internally under the clinic complaints procedure, confirm the correct contact details for you, and update you once the review is complete. We will also arrange a new appointment if you still want one.

Thank you for giving us the opportunity to respond.

Yours sincerely,
[Name]

## AI Skill 1 - Model Answer

A good student may use AI to propose a structure, but should revise it so that it matches the audience, the factual record, and the purpose of the communication. The student should check that the AI has not introduced new facts or legal conclusions.

## AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

A good student should identify and remove overstatement, blame language, premature threats, unsupported admissions, and vague drafting. AI suggestions should be treated as drafting options, not final judgment.

## AI Skill 3 - Model Answer

A good student should compare the AI fact/assumption list against all six exhibits. Confirmed facts should be separated from disputed points, inferred points, and missing evidence. Any hallucinated or unsupported AI output should be corrected.

## AI Section Completion Standard - Tutor Guidance

A complete answer shows that the student used AI to improve clarity and organisation while retaining human responsibility for accuracy, professional tone, fact checking, caveats, and final judgment.

# AI Simulation Agent Brief

Simulation Type: Clarification call before writing or live feedback on a written message.

Agent Role: Supervisor or client contact, depending on platform setup.

Agent Persona: A time-pressured stakeholder who wants a useful complaint response letter and is concerned about a missed appointment and wrongly addressed follow-up email.

Simulation Purpose: The student practises clarifying audience, purpose, tone, missing information, and unsafe wording before finalising the written communication.

Opening Statement: Thanks for looking at this. I need a clear written response, but I do not want us to say anything that creates unnecessary risk. What do you think we can safely say?

## Core Facts the Agent Knows

A private clinic received a complaint from a patient after a cancelled consultation and an email sent to the wrong address. The clinic wants a response that is empathetic, accurate, and careful about what is confirmed.

The final communication must be practical and evidence-led.

There are missing or disputed facts that should not be glossed over.

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character and do not give model answers.

Ask the student to justify wording by reference to the exhibits.

Push back if the student uses unsupported allegations or promises.

Reward clear, measured, audience-aware communication.

Do not invent new facts beyond the exhibits and brief.
