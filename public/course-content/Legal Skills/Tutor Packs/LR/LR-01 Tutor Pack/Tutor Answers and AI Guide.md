### Tutor Use Note

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibit documents.

The model answers indicate the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

## Matter Snapshot for Tutors

A client has suffered a data incident involving identity documents and payroll information. You are not asked to give final law advice. You must prepare a research plan and findings note identifying what applicable legal rules must be checked.

## Required Output Guidance

Research plan

Questions, sources to check, priority order and factual assumptions.

Evidence standard: Must avoid assuming jurisdiction.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Preliminary findings note

Notification issues, affected groups, uncertainty and next steps.

Evidence standard: Must distinguish research questions from facts.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Fact request list

Information needed before applying the law.

Evidence standard: Each request must link to a legal decision point.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

> AI Extension Model Answers - Three Scenario Questions

> Tutor assessment focus Tutors should assess only three AI questions. Strong answers must be grounded in LR-01 - Researching Data Breach Reporting Duties and the matching exhibit bundle, not generic AI risk language.

> - AI Task 1 - Human Skill First Strong answer identifies three priority issues from the exhibit bundle, explains why they matter for Legal Research, separates confirmed facts from gaps, and states what must be checked or escalated before AI is used.

> - AI Task 2 - AI Tool Usage and Critique Strong answer gives one safe prompt limited to LR-01 - Researching Data Breach Reporting Duties; asks for structure, critique or drafting support only; requires primary practical output, risk or issue list, reflection or improvement note; prohibits invented facts or assumed law; and explains how the output will be checked against exhibits and source reliability.

> - AI Task 3 - Judgment and AI Supervision Under Pressure Strong answer explains what the student would accept, change or reject from AI output; keeps caveats visible; identifies missing evidence and approval points; and produces a supervisor-ready position grounded in the scenario.
