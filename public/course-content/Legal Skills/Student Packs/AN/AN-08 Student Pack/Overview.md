# Scenario Title:

Preparing an Attendance Note After a Startup Founder Dispute Call

**Industry / Practice Area:** Technology / Startups

**Scenario type:** Client follow-up call and attendance note preparation

**Difficulty:** Intermediate

# Scenario Background:

Priya Nair is co-founder of a small workflow software startup. The firm has received materials about a dispute with a departing co-founder over code access and customer contacts. Priya says the departing co-founder is stealing the product, but the exhibits show unclear ownership records, shared tools, and possible unresolved founder arrangements. The supervising solicitor needs a clear attendance note and initial analysis before any advice, external communication, or urgent step is taken.

The scenario is jurisdiction-neutral. Focus on practical fact handling, careful recording, missing evidence, client objectives, communication risk, and escalation to the supervising solicitor.

# Student Role:

You are a trainee solicitor or junior legal assistant asked to prepare a professional attendance note for the supervising solicitor. You are not expected to give final advice. Your role is to organise the information, identify what is known and unknown, and prepare a clear record that supports sensible next steps.

# Documents / Information Available:

1. Exhibit 1 — Client Call Summary
1. Exhibit 2 — Repository Access Log
1. Exhibit 3 — Draft Founder Term Sheet
1. Exhibit 4 — Customer Email Extract
1. Exhibit 5 — Supervisor Instruction Note
1. Exhibit 6 — Draft Cease and Desist

# Core Skill Task:

Prepare a professional attendance note and initial legal analysis from mixed, incomplete, and potentially disputed client information.

# Main Practical Task:

Prepare a professional attendance note for the supervising solicitor. The note should record the client context, key facts, chronology, confirmed and uncertain points, issues, risks, missing evidence, communication concerns, and recommended next steps. It should be clear enough for the supervising solicitor to review before deciding what advice or external communication is appropriate.
