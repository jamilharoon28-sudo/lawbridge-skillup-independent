**LD-07 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS**

Scenario: Drafting a Temporary Pop-Up Licence Variation

Skill: Legal Drafting

Industry / Practice Area: Retail Property / Pop-Up Trading / Commercial Occupancy

# Task 1 — Model Answer

The drafting objective is to prepare a first working draft of the temporary licence variation letter for Market Yard Collective. The draft should support draft a temporary variation letter allowing limited extended trading hours and outdoor seating while preserving control over noise, cleaning, insurance, and revocation rights. It should be practical, evidence-based, and suitable for supervisor review rather than sending immediately. The purpose is not to make final legal findings, but to convert the client’s commercial objective into controlled wording that preserves position, records any conditions, and avoids unnecessary escalation.

# Task 2 — Model Answer

The draft should accurately identify Market Yard Collective, Juniper Coffee Co., the relevant client contact (Marta Singh, Asset Manager), and the matter context. It should use the document titles and dates from the exhibits, including the instruction document, the relevant contract or policy extract, and the correspondence or operational records. Any defined terms should be consistent throughout. If authority to sign, approve, vary, or send the document is unclear, the draft should flag that as a supervisor approval point rather than assume authority.

# Task 3 — Model Answer

Facts that can safely be used include: Juniper Coffee Co. has a pop-up licence at Market Yard; the operator wants extended hours and outdoor seating during a festival; neighbouring tenants have concerns about noise and cleaning. The draft should not present uncertain points as established facts. The following should be treated cautiously: whether the operator’s insurance covers outdoor seating; exact seating area dimensions; whether local permissions or landlord approvals are needed. The wording should say “our understanding is”, “the records indicate”, or “please confirm” where the evidence is incomplete. It should avoid phrases such as “you admit”, “you are solely responsible”, or “there is no dispute” unless the exhibits clearly support them.

# Task 4 — Model Answer

The drafting basis is the practical and contractual framework shown in the exhibits, not a jurisdiction-specific statute or legal test. The draft should rely on the relevant agreement, policy, instruction, or commercial process and should reflect any variation, notice, approval, cure, or authority requirements shown in the documents. Where the basis is not fully clear, the draft should preserve rights and state that the position is subject to review and supervisor confirmation.

# Task 5 — Model Answer

The intended outcome is to give Market Yard Collective a controlled written document that advances its objective while reducing drafting risk. The draft should include the key obligations, protections, conditions, reservations, or approvals required in this scenario. It should also preserve commercial flexibility where appropriate. The answer should recognise these risks: granting rights wider than intended; creating permanent variation accidentally; failing to include cleaning/noise controls. It should not overreach by demanding outcomes that the evidence or instructions do not yet support.

# Task 6 — Model Answer

Information to clarify includes: whether the operator’s insurance covers outdoor seating; exact seating area dimensions; whether local permissions or landlord approvals are needed. The student should also check final commercial instructions, names and signing authority, exact dates and deadlines, whether any internal or counterparty approval is required, and whether the supervisor wants express reservation-of-rights wording. Any operational promise in the draft should be checked with the client before inclusion.

# Task 7 — Model Answer

Unsafe wording would include language that makes admissions, asserts blame without evidence, assumes legal consequences, or threatens action that the contract/process may not allow. In this matter, the student should avoid: granting rights wider than intended; creating permanent variation accidentally; failing to include cleaning/noise controls. The safer approach is to use neutral, precise language: “the available records indicate”, “please provide”, “subject to our client’s rights”, “without admission”, “for review”, or “subject to approval”, depending on the draft.

# Task 8 — Model Answer

The document structure should start by identifying the parties, the purpose of the draft, and the relevant background. It should then set out the operative wording: obligations or commitments, dates, conditions, evidence or documents required, any reservation of rights, and next steps. The final section should make clear what is required from Juniper Coffee Co., what remains subject to approval, and who should respond. The order should make the document easy to use operationally.

# Task 9 — Model Answer

A strong draft would use concise operative wording. Example: “This draft is prepared on the basis of the information currently available and is subject to supervisor review. Our client’s position is reserved. The parties agree/are asked to confirm the following: [insert specific obligation], by [date], with supporting confirmation of [evidence], and without prejudice to any outstanding points identified in the correspondence.” The exact wording should be tailored to the document type and should not include unsupported admissions or threats.

# Task 10 — Model Answer

The final checklist should ask: have the parties and dates been checked; does the draft match the exhibits; are uncertain facts labelled as uncertain; is authority to send or sign confirmed; are all deadlines workable; are defined terms consistent; does the draft avoid jurisdiction-specific assumptions; are admissions and threats removed; are reservations and conditions included; and has the supervisor approved the final form before use.

# Main Practical Task — Full Model Answer

Draft Temporary Licence Variation

Market Yard agrees, subject to the conditions below, to a temporary variation of Juniper Coffee Co.’s pop-up licence for the period 21 July to 4 August 2027 only.

1. Extended hours are permitted only on the festival dates agreed in writing, and trading must end by the time specified by Market Yard.
2. Outdoor seating is limited to the marked area approved by Market Yard and must not obstruct entrances, access routes, emergency routes, or neighbouring displays.
3. Juniper must keep the area clean, remove furniture at close, manage customer noise, and comply with site rules.
4. Juniper must provide satisfactory insurance confirmation covering the temporary outdoor seating before use begins.
5. Market Yard may withdraw the temporary permission on reasonable notice if there are safety, access, noise, insurance, or cleaning concerns.
6. All other licence terms remain unchanged and this variation is not permanent.
