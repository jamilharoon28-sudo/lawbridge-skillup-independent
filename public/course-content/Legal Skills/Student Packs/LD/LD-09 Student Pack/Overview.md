*Jurisdiction-neutral practical drafting simulation*

**Scenario Title** Drafting a Confidentiality Undertaking for a Departing Consultant

**Industry / Practice Area** Professional Services / Consultancy / Confidential Information

**Client** Bluepeak Advisory LLP

**Counterparty / Other Party** Rafael Noor, departing consultant

**Core Drafting Output** confidentiality undertaking and return-of-materials wording

# Scenario Background:

Bluepeak Advisory LLP has discovered that a departing consultant forwarded several project files to a personal email address shortly before leaving. The consultant says he did this to work from home and did not misuse the files. The client wants an undertaking before final exit payments are processed. The evidence shows concern but not confirmed misuse. The drafting task is to produce firm, practical undertaking wording focused on return, deletion, confirmation, and preservation of rights.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal professional assisting Oscar Lane, supervising solicitor. You have been asked to prepare the first working draft of the confidentiality undertaking and return-of-materials wording. You are not expected to give final legal advice or send the document. Your role is to organise the facts, identify drafting risks, prepare clear wording, and highlight points requiring supervisor approval.

# Documents / Information Available:

1. Exhibit 1 — People Partner instruction
2. Exhibit 2 — Consultancy agreement extract
3. Exhibit 3 — IT forwarding report
4. Exhibit 4 — Consultant explanation email
5. Exhibit 5 — Project confidentiality summary
6. Exhibit 6 — Overbroad draft undertaking

# Core Skill Task:

Use the documents to prepare a clear, evidence-based, proportionate legal draft that reflects the client’s instructions while avoiding unsupported admissions, overstatement, ambiguity, and jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

# Main Practical Task:

Draft an undertaking requiring return or deletion of confidential materials and sensible restrictions on use, while avoiding excessive or unsupported allegations.
