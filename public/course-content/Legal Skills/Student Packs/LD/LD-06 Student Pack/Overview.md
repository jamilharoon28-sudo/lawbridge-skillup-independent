*Jurisdiction-neutral practical drafting simulation*

**Scenario Title** Drafting a Customer Complaint Resolution Letter for Faulty Furniture

**Industry / Practice Area** Retail / E-commerce / Consumer Complaints

**Client** Fern & Finch Living Ltd

**Counterparty / Other Party** Customer: Tomas Varga

**Core Drafting Output** customer complaint resolution letter

# Scenario Background:

Fern & Finch Living Ltd sells furniture online. A customer says a dining table arrived damaged and that customer support ignored him. The client wants to protect its reputation and resolve the complaint, but internal records show the delivery photos are incomplete and the customer may have assembled the item before reporting damage. The drafting task is to prepare a professional complaint response that offers a practical resolution while keeping factual caveats.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal professional assisting Isha Patel, supervising solicitor. You have been asked to prepare the first working draft of the customer complaint resolution letter. You are not expected to give final legal advice or send the document. Your role is to organise the facts, identify drafting risks, prepare clear wording, and highlight points requiring supervisor approval.

# Documents / Information Available:

1. Exhibit 1 — Customer operations instruction
2. Exhibit 2 — Customer complaint email
3. Exhibit 3 — Order and delivery record
4. Exhibit 4 — Warehouse photo summary
5. Exhibit 5 — Customer service chat extract
6. Exhibit 6 — Aggressive draft response

# Core Skill Task:

Use the documents to prepare a clear, evidence-based, proportionate legal draft that reflects the client’s instructions while avoiding unsupported admissions, overstatement, ambiguity, and jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

# Main Practical Task:

Draft a clear and proportionate customer resolution letter offering practical remedies while avoiding unsupported admissions or jurisdiction-specific consumer law statements.
