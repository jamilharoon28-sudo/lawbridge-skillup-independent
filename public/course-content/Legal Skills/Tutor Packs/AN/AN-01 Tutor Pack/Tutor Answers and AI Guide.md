**Tutor Use Note**

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibit documents.

The model answers indicate the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

**Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.**

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

**Matter Snapshot for Tutors**

A company has suffered a suspected phishing-led cyberattack. You have interview notes, technical extracts and internal communications. Your task is to prepare a clear attendance note, legal analysis and evidence request list.

**Required Output Guidance**

Attendance note

Participants, date/time, instructions, confirmed facts, advice given, caveats and next steps.

Evidence standard: Must cite exhibits and avoid unsupported conclusions.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Legal analysis note

Risk areas, applicable legal verification points, evidence gaps and recommended immediate action.

Evidence standard: Separate legal issues from operational assumptions.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Evidence request list

Information needed from IT, management, vendors, insurance and communications.

Evidence standard: Each request must explain why it is needed.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

**Indicative Marking Rubric**

| **Criterion**         | **What to reward**                                                    | **Weight** |
|-----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------|------------|

| Structure and clarity | Produces a usable trainee-style output, not an essay.                 | 20%        |

| AI supervision        | Uses AI only after human issue-mapping and critiques output properly. | 15%        |
