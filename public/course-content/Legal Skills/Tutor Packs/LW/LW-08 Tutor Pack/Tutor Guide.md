# LW-08 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS

## Task 1 - Model Answer

The audience is the supplier counterparty. The purpose is to produce a settlement proposal email that helps the reader understand the position, practical risk, and next step. The response should be commercial, constructive, accurate, and solution-focused and should not go beyond the evidence.

## Task 2 - Model Answer

Key facts include: BrightJar asks for a settlement email to GlassPro Supplies; April order: 50,000 jars ordered, 41,000 delivered; The supply terms require reasonable notice of increased volumes and prompt notice of delivery discrepancies. The facts should be presented in a logical order and tied back to the main written response rather than listed without analysis.

## Task 3 - Model Answer

The issues are factual uncertainty, communication risk, evidence gaps, and the need to write in a way that is useful without overstating the legal position. The student should identify both the supported concerns and the limits of the evidence.

## Task 4 - Model Answer

The client objective is to write a settlement email that is firm but relationship-preserving. The written response should advance that objective while preserving credibility, avoiding unnecessary escalation, and leaving room for supervisor review.

## Task 5 - Model Answer

The communication must explain the supported facts, the practical significance of those facts, the points that remain uncertain, and the recommended next steps. It should be specific enough to be useful and simple enough for the intended reader.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The student should avoid unsupported allegations, definitive conclusions not proved by the exhibits, unnecessary admissions, threats that have not been approved, and language that may damage credibility. In this scenario, avoid: threatening to terminate immediately; claiming exact losses without finance confirmation; ignoring forecast changes; using hostile wording that damages the relationship.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Important caveats include the lack of complete evidence, unresolved factual disputes, and the need for supervisor review before making stronger assertions. The communication should distinguish known facts from allegations, assumptions, and points requiring follow-up.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible structure is: short introduction, confirmed factual summary, key issue or risk, caveats/missing evidence, practical recommendation, and next steps. The tone should be commercial, constructive, accurate, and solution-focused.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Key points should include: what delivery shortfall is supported; business impact without overstating quantified loss; client’s preferred settlement options; request for documents and future supply assurances. These should be drafted in complete, audience-appropriate sentences rather than as internal notes.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final communication should be accurate, concise, and usable. It should address the reader directly, include only supported facts, make uncertainty clear, and end with an actionable next step.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Subject: April and May jar delivery shortfalls - proposed resolution

Dear [Name],

We are writing about the April and May jar deliveries under our ongoing supply arrangement. BrightJar values the relationship with GlassPro and would like to resolve the issue commercially while ensuring future supply is stable.

The April order appears straightforward: 50,000 jars were ordered and 41,000 were delivered. We note your offer to credit the 9,000-jar shortfall. Please confirm the amount and timing of that credit.

The May position needs further reconciliation. BrightJar’s records show 60,000 ordered and 48,500 received. Your email refers to 52,000 loaded and a late order amendment. Please provide the loading records, delivery notes, and any order amendment record you rely on so that the figures can be checked.

BrightJar has experienced production disruption, although the precise loss calculation is still being reviewed. For that reason, we are not treating the preliminary finance figure as final. We would like to explore a practical resolution including: (1) confirmed credit for the April shortfall; (2) reconciliation of the May delivery records; (3) an agreed service plan for June and July volumes; and (4) a commercial credit or priority supply arrangement once the records are reconciled.

Please respond by [date] so we can resolve this without disrupting future orders.

Kind regards,
[Name]

## AI Skill 1 - Model Answer

A good student may use AI to propose a structure, but should revise it so that it matches the audience, the factual record, and the purpose of the communication. The student should check that the AI has not introduced new facts or legal conclusions.

## AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

A good student should identify and remove overstatement, blame language, premature threats, unsupported admissions, and vague drafting. AI suggestions should be treated as drafting options, not final judgment.

## AI Skill 3 - Model Answer

A good student should compare the AI fact/assumption list against all six exhibits. Confirmed facts should be separated from disputed points, inferred points, and missing evidence. Any hallucinated or unsupported AI output should be corrected.

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character and do not give model answers.

Ask the student to justify wording by reference to the exhibits.

Push back if the student uses unsupported allegations or promises.

Reward clear, measured, audience-aware communication.

Do not invent new facts beyond the exhibits and brief.
