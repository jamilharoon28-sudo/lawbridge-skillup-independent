*Jurisdiction-neutral practical drafting simulation*

**Scenario Title** Drafting a Private Clinic Complaint Response and Records Request Letter

**Industry / Practice Area** Healthcare Services / Private Clinic / Complaint Handling

**Client** Riverton Wellness Clinic

**Counterparty / Other Party** Patient: Clara Jain

**Core Drafting Output** patient complaint response and records request acknowledgement

# Scenario Background:

Riverton Wellness Clinic received a complaint from a patient who says her appointment was cancelled at short notice and that a receptionist discussed her appointment where others could hear. The clinic director wants to apologise and resolve the matter quickly, but records are incomplete and staff accounts differ. The drafting task is to prepare a sensitive response that acknowledges the complaint, explains the clinic’s review process in neutral terms, and asks for any missing details.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal professional assisting Ava Turner, supervising solicitor. You have been asked to prepare the first working draft of the patient complaint response and records request acknowledgement. You are not expected to give final legal advice or send the document. Your role is to organise the facts, identify drafting risks, prepare clear wording, and highlight points requiring supervisor approval.

# Documents / Information Available:

1. Exhibit 1 — Clinic director instruction
2. Exhibit 2 — Patient complaint email
3. Exhibit 3 — Appointment system extract
4. Exhibit 4 — Reception staff note
5. Exhibit 5 — Clinic complaints policy extract
6. Exhibit 6 — Draft response prepared by receptionist

# Core Skill Task:

Use the documents to prepare a clear, evidence-based, proportionate legal draft that reflects the client’s instructions while avoiding unsupported admissions, overstatement, ambiguity, and jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

# Main Practical Task:

Draft a careful patient complaint response acknowledging concerns, requesting clarification, preserving confidentiality, and setting out next practical steps without making unsupported admissions.
