*Jurisdiction-neutral practical drafting simulation*

**Scenario Title** Drafting a Board Authority Resolution for a Distribution Agreement

**Industry / Practice Area** Manufacturing / Distribution / Corporate Administration

**Client** Atlas Cycle Components Ltd

**Counterparty / Other Party** Northline Distribution GmbH

**Core Drafting Output** board authority resolution and signing authority note

# Scenario Background:

Atlas Cycle Components Ltd is negotiating a distribution agreement for a new range of cycle parts. The finance director needs board authority wording before signature. The contract is nearly agreed, but there are outstanding points on minimum order volumes and exclusivity. The drafting task is to produce a clear corporate authority resolution that does not approve unresolved commercial terms beyond the board’s intention.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal professional assisting Nina Brooks, supervising solicitor. You have been asked to prepare the first working draft of the board authority resolution and signing authority note. You are not expected to give final legal advice or send the document. Your role is to organise the facts, identify drafting risks, prepare clear wording, and highlight points requiring supervisor approval.

# Core Skill Task:

Use the documents to prepare a clear, evidence-based, proportionate legal draft that reflects the client’s instructions while avoiding unsupported admissions, overstatement, ambiguity, and jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

# Main Practical Task:

Draft board authority wording approving entry into a distribution agreement and authorising signature, subject to final commercial terms and supervisor review.
