*Jurisdiction-neutral practical drafting simulation*

**Scenario Title** Drafting a Temporary Pop-Up Licence Variation

**Industry / Practice Area** Retail Property / Pop-Up Trading / Commercial Occupancy

**Client** Market Yard Collective

**Counterparty / Other Party** Juniper Coffee Co.

**Core Drafting Output** temporary licence variation letter

# Scenario Background:

Market Yard Collective manages a mixed-use retail yard. A pop-up coffee operator wants extended evening hours and temporary outdoor seating during a summer arts festival. Market Yard is open to the commercial opportunity, but neighbouring tenants have raised noise and cleaning concerns. The existing licence is short and does not cover outdoor seating. The drafting task is to prepare a temporary variation that is practical, conditional, and easy to administer.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal professional assisting Theo Malik, supervising solicitor. You have been asked to prepare the first working draft of the temporary licence variation letter. You are not expected to give final legal advice or send the document. Your role is to organise the facts, identify drafting risks, prepare clear wording, and highlight points requiring supervisor approval.

# Documents / Information Available:

1. Exhibit 1 — Asset manager instruction
2. Exhibit 2 — Existing pop-up licence extract
3. Exhibit 3 — Festival event schedule
4. Exhibit 4 — Neighbouring tenant complaint email
5. Exhibit 5 — Insurance summary
6. Exhibit 6 — Coffee operator proposed wording

# Core Skill Task:

Use the documents to prepare a clear, evidence-based, proportionate legal draft that reflects the client’s instructions while avoiding unsupported admissions, overstatement, ambiguity, and jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

# Main Practical Task:

Draft a temporary variation letter allowing limited extended trading hours and outdoor seating while preserving control over noise, cleaning, insurance, and revocation rights.
