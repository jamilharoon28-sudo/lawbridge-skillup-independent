**Tutor Use Note**

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibit documents.

The model answers indicate the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

**Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.**

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

**Matter Snapshot for Tutors**

A small business is about to sign a settlement agreement after a service dispute. The client wants a quick check of the draft agreement, payment schedule and confidentiality wording.

**Required Output Guidance**

Pre-signature review checklist

Parties, dates, payment, obligations, release, confidentiality and signature blocks.

Evidence standard: Must be systematic.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Issue list for supervisor

Red flags and questions before signature.

Evidence standard: Must be concise.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Client clarification questions

Plain English questions for the client.

Evidence standard: Must not give final advice without approval.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.
