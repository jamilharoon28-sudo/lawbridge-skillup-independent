### Tutor Use Note

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibit documents.

The model answers indicate the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

## Matter Snapshot for Tutors

A buyer wants to acquire a cloud software company. The file reveals unclear intellectual property ownership, contractor-created code, open-source software, employee retention issues and regulatory investigations. You must prepare a matter analysis and red flag report.

## Required Output Guidance

Red flag report

Top risks, evidence, impact, priority and recommended protection.

Evidence standard: Must cite exhibits.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Due diligence request list

Follow-up documents/questions by risk area.

Evidence standard: Each request must link to completion risk.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Supervisor strategy note

Deal impact, protections, escalation and negotiation points.

Evidence standard: Must prioritise, not list everything equally.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

> AI Extension Model Answers - Three Scenario Questions

> Tutor assessment focus Tutors should assess only three AI questions. Strong answers must be grounded in CMA-01 - Analysing Red Flags in a Tech Company Acquisition and the matching exhibit bundle, not generic AI risk language.

> - AI Task 1 - Human Skill First Strong answer identifies three priority issues from the exhibit bundle, explains why they matter for Case and Matter Analysis, separates confirmed facts from gaps, and states what must be checked or escalated before AI is used.

> - AI Task 2 - AI Tool Usage and Critique Strong answer gives one safe prompt limited to CMA-01 - Analysing Red Flags in a Tech Company Acquisition; asks for structure, critique or drafting support only; requires primary practical output, risk or issue list, reflection or improvement note; prohibits invented facts or assumed law; and explains how the output will be checked against exhibits and source reliability.

> - AI Task 3 - Judgment and AI Supervision Under Pressure Strong answer explains what the student would accept, change or reject from AI output; keeps caveats visible; identifies missing evidence and approval points; and produces a supervisor-ready position grounded in the scenario.

| Common weaknesses to mark down |
| --- |
| Generic AI commentary; asking AI for the final answer; failure to cite or check exhibits; hiding uncertainty; no supervisor escalation where facts, law or approvals remain unresolved. |
