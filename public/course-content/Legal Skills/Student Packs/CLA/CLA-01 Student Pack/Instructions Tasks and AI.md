## How to Use This Simulation


● Read the scenario and exhibits as if this is a training file, not a real client matter.

● Do not rely on headings as the answer. Extract the important facts, risks and missing information yourself.

● Use short, clear sentences. Plain English is preferred.

● Separate what is known, what is assumed, what is disputed and what still needs checking.

● Prepare a practical output for a supervisor, client or mentor using only the information in the file.

● Do not give a final legal conclusion without checking the applicable legal framework.


## Learning Outcomes


● Analyse a commercial opportunity through legal risk.

● Separate business value from legal uncertainty.

● Identify data, brand, operational and exit risks.

● Prepare a practical risk note for a non-lawyer decision-maker.

● Recommend next steps without blocking the business unnecessarily.


## Student Task Brief


A retail client wants to share customer insight data with a loyalty app partner. The business sees marketing value, but the file raises data, reputation, control and termination concerns.

## Working Method


● Start with the exhibit index, but do not treat it as a summary of the answer.

● Mark every clause, date, figure and disputed factual point that affects advice or drafting.

● Separate confirmed facts, disputed facts, assumptions, missing information and legal points requiring further checking.

● Keep supervisor-facing outputs concise: lead with the practical risk, then the evidence, then the next step.
