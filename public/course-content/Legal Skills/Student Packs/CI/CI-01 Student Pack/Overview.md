# CI-01 | CLIENT INTERVIEWING

LAWBRIDGE PRACTICAL SKILLS COURSE

**Scenario Title** Initial Client Interview About a Disputed Home Renovation Project

**Industry / Practice Area** Consumer Contracts / Home Renovation / Client Interviewing

**Difficulty** Intermediate

**Scenario Count** 1

**Jurisdiction** Jurisdiction-neutral

# Scenario Background

Amina Patel has contacted the LawBridge Legal Clinic about a home renovation project that has become difficult and stressful. She instructed BrightBuild Renovations Ltd to renovate her kitchen and dining area. She says the work was late, some items remain defective, and the contractor is pressing her to pay a final invoice that includes an additional pantry variation charge. She also signed a completion form but says she felt put under pressure and did not understand whether it meant accepting the work as complete.

The supervising solicitor has asked you to prepare for and conduct an initial client interview. The purpose is to gather facts, clarify the client’s objectives, identify documents and evidence, manage communication risk, and prepare a short post-interview summary. You must not give final legal advice during the interview. The situation requires careful client handling because Ms Patel is anxious, the payment deadline is close, and she is considering posting photos online.

# Student Role

You are acting as a trainee solicitor / junior legal assistant supporting the supervising solicitor. Your role is to prepare for the initial client interview, conduct the interview professionally, ask appropriate questions, identify missing information, avoid premature advice, and prepare a post-interview summary for supervisor review.

# Core Skill Task

Prepare for, conduct, and close a structured client interview in a way that gathers reliable facts, manages the client’s concerns, and creates a useful post-interview summary for the supervising solicitor.

# Main Practical Task

Conduct a structured client interview with Ms Patel and produce a short post-interview summary for the supervising solicitor. Your summary should identify the client’s objectives, confirmed facts, uncertain or disputed facts, evidence needed, communication risks, and recommended next steps.
