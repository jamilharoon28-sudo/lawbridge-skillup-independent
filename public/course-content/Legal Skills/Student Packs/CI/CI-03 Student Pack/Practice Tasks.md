# Detailed Practice Tasks

### Task 1 — Identify the client, matter, and interview purpose

Identify who the client is, what matter they are bringing, what they want from the interview, and why the interview must be structured rather than treated as a quick advice call.

### Task 2 — Extract what is already known from the documents

Using the six exhibits, list the facts already available about a former contractor claims ownership of key code days before an investor demo. Separate source-backed facts from the client’s own account.

### Task 3 — Identify what is missing or unclear

Identify the documents, dates, people, communications, and factual details that remain missing, unclear, disputed, or unsupported.

### Task 4 — Prepare open questions

Prepare open questions that allow Leo Patel to explain the background in their own words without being led to a particular answer.

### Task 5 — Prepare focused follow-up questions

Prepare focused follow-up questions about dates, documents, conversations, missing evidence, and the practical outcome sought.

### Task 6 — Identify sensitive issues and how to ask about them

Identify the sensitive issues that may make the client emotional, defensive, embarrassed, or anxious, and draft careful wording for asking about them.

### Task 7 — Identify legal and professional boundaries

Explain what the interviewer should and should not say during the interview, especially where the facts are incomplete or the client asks for immediate advice.

### Task 8 — Plan explanation of role, confidentiality, and next steps

Prepare a plain-language opening that explains the interviewer’s role, the purpose of the meeting, confidentiality limits in general terms, and what will happen after the interview.

### Task 9 — Plan how to handle difficult emotions or inconsistent answers

Identify likely difficult moments in the interview and explain how the interviewer should respond while keeping the discussion focused and respectful.

### Task 10 — Prepare a structured interview plan

Prepare a clear interview structure covering opening, background questions, document review, missing evidence, client objectives, risk points, summary, and next steps.
