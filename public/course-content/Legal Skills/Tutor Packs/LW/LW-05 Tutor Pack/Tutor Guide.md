# LW-05 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS

## Task 1 - Model Answer

The audience is the client board. The purpose is to produce a board briefing that helps the reader understand the position, practical risk, and next step. The response should be business-facing, concise, neutral, and decision-focused and should not go beyond the evidence.

## Task 2 - Model Answer

Key facts include: The board asks for a briefing on whether Oakline can terminate its system implementation contract with SyncFlow after a failed go-live; Go-live was planned for 1 September; The agreement requires both parties to follow a remediation plan before termination for delay. The facts should be presented in a logical order and tied back to the main written response rather than listed without analysis.

## Task 3 - Model Answer

The issues are factual uncertainty, communication risk, evidence gaps, and the need to write in a way that is useful without overstating the legal position. The student should identify both the supported concerns and the limits of the evidence.

## Task 4 - Model Answer

The client objective is to prepare a balanced board briefing that explains risks, options, and information gaps. The written response should advance that objective while preserving credibility, avoiding unnecessary escalation, and leaving room for supervisor review.

## Task 5 - Model Answer

The communication must explain the supported facts, the practical significance of those facts, the points that remain uncertain, and the recommended next steps. It should be specific enough to be useful and simple enough for the intended reader.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The student should avoid unsupported allegations, definitive conclusions not proved by the exhibits, unnecessary admissions, threats that have not been approved, and language that may damage credibility. In this scenario, avoid: telling the board termination is risk-free; blaming the vendor entirely where records are mixed; using legal jargon without explanation; omitting operational impact and missing evidence.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Important caveats include the lack of complete evidence, unresolved factual disputes, and the need for supervisor review before making stronger assertions. The communication should distinguish known facts from allegations, assumptions, and points requiring follow-up.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible structure is: short introduction, confirmed factual summary, key issue or risk, caveats/missing evidence, practical recommendation, and next steps. The tone should be business-facing, concise, neutral, and decision-focused.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Key points should include: what is known about missed rollout milestones; vendor and client contributions to delay; contractual process risk in neutral terms; options short of immediate termination. These should be drafted in complete, audience-appropriate sentences rather than as internal notes.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final communication should be accurate, concise, and usable. It should address the reader directly, include only supported facts, make uncertainty clear, and end with an actionable next step.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Board briefing - SyncFlow rollout failure

Issue: Oakline needs to decide how to respond to the failed stock system rollout. The board should not treat immediate termination as risk-free on the documents currently available.

What is known: The planned 1 September go-live did not happen. The 29 August pilot failed in part because of missing store data and incomplete stock mapping. The 12 September revised test also failed, apparently linked to courier integration and returns data. SyncFlow accepts some development delay but says Oakline added integration and analytics requirements late and missed data deadlines for six stores. Internal notes support that Oakline had data quality problems.

Contract process risk: The implementation agreement appears to require a remediation plan before termination for delay. It also identifies client dependencies, including accurate data, user testing, and written scope changes. This means Oakline should be cautious about sending an immediate termination notice before checking whether contractual steps have been followed.

Options: (1) require SyncFlow to produce a formal remediation plan with dates and ownership; (2) reserve Oakline’s position on delay and losses while gathering data evidence; (3) negotiate a revised delivery plan, fee holdback, or exit route; or (4) consider termination only after supervisor review of the contract, notices, and evidence.

Recommended next step: Send a controlled letter seeking remediation, documents, and a revised plan, while preserving rights. Do not send the current draft slide wording as an external communication.

## AI Skill 1 - Model Answer

A good student may use AI to propose a structure, but should revise it so that it matches the audience, the factual record, and the purpose of the communication. The student should check that the AI has not introduced new facts or legal conclusions.

## AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

A good student should identify and remove overstatement, blame language, premature threats, unsupported admissions, and vague drafting. AI suggestions should be treated as drafting options, not final judgment.

## AI Skill 3 - Model Answer

A good student should compare the AI fact/assumption list against all six exhibits. Confirmed facts should be separated from disputed points, inferred points, and missing evidence. Any hallucinated or unsupported AI output should be corrected.

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character and do not give model answers.

Ask the student to justify wording by reference to the exhibits.

Push back if the student uses unsupported allegations or promises.

Reward clear, measured, audience-aware communication.

Do not invent new facts beyond the exhibits and brief.
