# Detailed Practice Tasks

## Task 1 - Identify the research question

Identify the precise research question the supervisor needs answered and the practical decision the research must support.

## Task 2 - Identify the factual context

Summarise the facts that matter for the research question, separating confirmed facts from assumptions or allegations.

## Task 3 - Break the question into sub-issues

Break the research question into focused sub-issues that can be answered using the provided source extracts and factual exhibits.

## Task 4 - Identify relevant sources

Identify which source extracts are most relevant and explain why they are useful.

## Task 5 - Assess source usefulness and reliability

Assess the relative usefulness and reliability of the sources, noting any limitations, conflicts, or gaps.

## Task 6 - Extract key principles

Extract the key legal or practical principles from the source extracts in neutral, jurisdiction-neutral terms.

## Task 7 - Apply the principles to the facts

Apply the extracted principles to the client facts without overstating certainty.

## Task 8 - Identify uncertainty and further research

Identify factual uncertainty, missing documents, and any further research or supervisor review needed.

## Task 9 - Prepare a provisional conclusion

Prepare a short provisional conclusion that answers the research question and flags caveats.

## Task 10 - Structure the research note

Plan the final research note so it is clear, practical, and useful to the supervisor.
