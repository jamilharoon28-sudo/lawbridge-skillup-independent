# LW-06 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS

## Task 1 - Model Answer

The audience is the tenant client. The purpose is to produce a client update email that helps the reader understand the position, practical risk, and next step. The response should be supportive, practical, clear, and careful and should not go beyond the evidence.

## Task 2 - Model Answer

Key facts include: Rosa asks for a plain-English update; Photos from 9 January, 2 February, and 19 March show staining near the window and peeling paint; The tenancy requires the landlord to keep structure and installations in reasonable repair and requires the tenant to allow reasonable access for inspection and works after notice. The facts should be presented in a logical order and tied back to the main written response rather than listed without analysis.

## Task 3 - Model Answer

The issues are factual uncertainty, communication risk, evidence gaps, and the need to write in a way that is useful without overstating the legal position. The student should identify both the supported concerns and the limits of the evidence.

## Task 4 - Model Answer

The client objective is to write a clear client update explaining next steps and managing expectations. The written response should advance that objective while preserving credibility, avoiding unnecessary escalation, and leaving room for supervisor review.

## Task 5 - Model Answer

The communication must explain the supported facts, the practical significance of those facts, the points that remain uncertain, and the recommended next steps. It should be specific enough to be useful and simple enough for the intended reader.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The student should avoid unsupported allegations, definitive conclusions not proved by the exhibits, unnecessary admissions, threats that have not been approved, and language that may damage credibility. In this scenario, avoid: guaranteeing compensation; telling the client to stop paying rent; accusing the landlord of deliberate harm; ignoring access and ventilation issues.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Important caveats include the lack of complete evidence, unresolved factual disputes, and the need for supervisor review before making stronger assertions. The communication should distinguish known facts from allegations, assumptions, and points requiring follow-up.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible structure is: short introduction, confirmed factual summary, key issue or risk, caveats/missing evidence, practical recommendation, and next steps. The tone should be supportive, practical, clear, and careful.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Key points should include: what evidence supports the complaint; what remains unclear about cause and access; why stopping rent without advice is risky; what further evidence and next steps are needed. These should be drafted in complete, audience-appropriate sentences rather than as internal notes.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final communication should be accurate, concise, and usable. It should address the reader directly, include only supported facts, make uncertainty clear, and end with an actionable next step.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Dear Rosa,

Thank you for sending the photographs and message history. I understand why you are concerned, particularly given your son’s asthma.

The documents help show that you reported damp and mould and that there are visible signs of staining and peeling paint near the bedroom window. The messages also show that the managing agent was aware of the issue and that an inspection visit was proposed.

There are still some points we need to clarify before making stronger allegations. The current evidence does not yet confirm the cause of the damp, whether there is an underlying repair issue, or whether condensation is contributing. We also need to deal carefully with the missed contractor visit. You say you were not given reasonable notice, while the agent says access was not available.

I would not recommend stopping rent without specific advice from the supervising solicitor. That could create a separate risk and may distract from the repair complaint.

The next practical step is to write to the managing agent asking for a prompt inspection, confirmation of the proposed works, and copies of any contractor reports. Please also send any GP or health appointment notes, any further photos, and any message showing when you were told about the February visit.

Kind regards,
[Name]

## AI Skill 1 - Model Answer

A good student may use AI to propose a structure, but should revise it so that it matches the audience, the factual record, and the purpose of the communication. The student should check that the AI has not introduced new facts or legal conclusions.

## AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

A good student should identify and remove overstatement, blame language, premature threats, unsupported admissions, and vague drafting. AI suggestions should be treated as drafting options, not final judgment.

## AI Skill 3 - Model Answer

A good student should compare the AI fact/assumption list against all six exhibits. Confirmed facts should be separated from disputed points, inferred points, and missing evidence. Any hallucinated or unsupported AI output should be corrected.

## AI Section Completion Standard - Tutor Guidance

## Core Facts the Agent Knows

A tenant wants an update after sending photographs and message records about damp. The client is anxious and wants to stop paying rent, but the documents show uncertainty about cause, access, and medical evidence.

The final communication must be practical and evidence-led.

There are missing or disputed facts that should not be glossed over.

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character and do not give model answers.

Ask the student to justify wording by reference to the exhibits.

Push back if the student uses unsupported allegations or promises.

Reward clear, measured, audience-aware communication.

Do not invent new facts beyond the exhibits and brief.
