*Jurisdiction-neutral practical drafting simulation*

**Scenario Title** Drafting a Remediation Notice for Commercial Cleaning Failures

**Industry / Practice Area** Property Management / Facilities Services

**Client** Westvale Property Management

**Counterparty / Other Party** BrightPath Cleaning Ltd

**Core Drafting Output** contractual remediation notice

# Scenario Background:

Westvale Property Management manages a serviced office building with premium tenants. BrightPath Cleaning Ltd provides evening cleaning services. Tenants have complained about missed kitchen cleaning and uncollected waste. Omar Linden wants to terminate the contract immediately, but the contract extract shows a staged remediation process before termination. The evidence also shows that building access cards failed on two evenings. The drafting task is to prepare a clear remediation notice that is firm, fact-based, and procedurally safe.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal professional assisting Amelia Grant, supervising solicitor. You have been asked to prepare the first working draft of the contractual remediation notice. You are not expected to give final legal advice or send the document. Your role is to organise the facts, identify drafting risks, prepare clear wording, and highlight points requiring supervisor approval.

# Documents / Information Available:

1. Exhibit 1 — Facilities manager instruction note
2. Exhibit 2 — Cleaning services agreement extract
3. Exhibit 3 — Tenant complaint log
4. Exhibit 4 — Access control incident report
5. Exhibit 5 — BrightPath response email
6. Exhibit 6 — Client draft termination email

# Core Skill Task:

Use the documents to prepare a clear, evidence-based, proportionate legal draft that reflects the client’s instructions while avoiding unsupported admissions, overstatement, ambiguity, and jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

# Main Practical Task:

Draft a remediation notice requiring BrightPath to correct cleaning service issues while preserving Westvale’s contractual position and avoiding premature termination wording.
