*Jurisdiction-neutral practical drafting simulation*

**Scenario Title** Drafting a Payment Plan Side Letter for a Construction Subcontractor

**Industry / Practice Area** Construction / Subcontracting / Commercial Payment

**Client** Stonebridge Fit-Out Ltd

**Counterparty / Other Party** Vale Mechanical Services

**Core Drafting Output** payment plan side letter

# Scenario Background:

Stonebridge Fit-Out Ltd is managing a refurbishment project. A subcontractor, Vale Mechanical Services, says it will slow down works unless two disputed invoices are paid. Stonebridge accepts part of the debt but disputes delay-related charges. The project deadline is tight and the client wants a side letter that keeps the subcontractor on site without conceding all disputed sums. The drafting task is to record a commercially workable payment plan that is clear, conditional, and without prejudice to unresolved issues.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal professional assisting Lewis Chen, supervising solicitor. You have been asked to prepare the first working draft of the payment plan side letter. You are not expected to give final legal advice or send the document. Your role is to organise the facts, identify drafting risks, prepare clear wording, and highlight points requiring supervisor approval.

Use the documents to prepare a clear, evidence-based, proportionate legal draft that reflects the client’s instructions while avoiding unsupported admissions, overstatement, ambiguity, and jurisdiction-specific assumptions.

Draft a side letter recording a staged payment arrangement, reservation of rights, and practical conditions for continued site performance.
