*Analysing a Charity Governance and Restricted Funds Concern*

**Scenario Title** Analysing a Charity Governance and Restricted Funds Concern

**Industry / Practice Area** Charities / Governance / Internal Investigation

**Difficulty** Intermediate

**Scenario Type** Case and matter analysis simulation

# Scenario Background

Northmere Community Trust has asked for help with use of restricted grant money for emergency operating costs. Hannah Patel, Trustee Chair, wants the legal team to help analyse the file and prepare an internal risk and next steps note. The trust is short of cash and the chair wants reassurance before a funder monitoring meeting. The available file is incomplete and contains conflicting accounts, so the student must analyse the matter carefully before any external communication or recommendation is made.

Central tension: Finance notes say the funds were only moved temporarily, but bank records show invoices paid from the restricted account. The matter cannot be resolved by accepting either side's account without further evidence.

# Student Role

You are acting as a junior legal professional in the team. You report to Olivia Grant. You are not expected to make final strategic decisions or send external correspondence. Your task is to analyse the matter file, organise the evidence, identify strengths and weaknesses, and prepare a clear note for supervisor review.

# Documents / Information Available

1. Exhibit 1 - File Opening Note and Client Objective
1. Exhibit 2 - Chronology of Key Events
1. Exhibit 3 - Key Correspondence Extract
1. Exhibit 4 - Relevant Agreement or Policy Extract
1. Exhibit 5 - Conflicting Evidence Summary
1. Exhibit 6 - Supervisor Instruction and Risk Note

# Core Skill Task

Analyse a realistic matter file, identify the key issues and evidence gaps, and prepare a practical case/matter analysis note for a supervising solicitor or matter lead.

# Main Practical Task

Prepare a concise case/matter analysis note for Olivia Grant. Your note should summarise the client objective, key facts, issue list, strengths, weaknesses, evidence gaps, communication risks, recommended next steps, and decisions requiring supervisor input.
