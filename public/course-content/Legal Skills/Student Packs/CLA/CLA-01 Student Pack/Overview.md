# Scenario Title:

Assessing a Warehouse Robotics Pilot Before Contract Expansion

# Industry / Practice Area:

Retail operations / warehouse automation / commercial contracts

# Scenario Background:

Oakridge Homeware Group is considering whether to expand a pilot robotics agreement to three regional warehouses. The potential value or exposure is approximately USD 1.8 million expansion spend. The commercial team wants a practical answer by the Friday board update. The business sees opportunity, but the materials show legal, operational, financial, and communication risks. The key tension is that the pilot results are promising but incomplete and the service data has not been independently checked The learner must prepare commercial legal analysis that helps the business decide what to do next without overstating the evidence or ignoring commercial priorities.

# Student Role:

You are a junior legal team member assisting the supervising solicitor. You have been asked to review the documents, identify the legal and commercial risks, prepare a business-facing analysis, and support a discussion with the business stakeholder. You are not expected to make the final business decision.

1. Exhibit 1 - Business Instruction Email

1. Exhibit 3 - Operational Data and Timeline Note

# Core Skill Task:

Analyse a commercial problem through a legal lens and translate the risks into practical business options, safeguards, and next steps.

# Main Practical Task:

Prepare a commercial legal risk note for Nadia Reeves, explaining the key risks, options, safeguards, missing evidence, and recommended next steps on warehouse robotics pilot expansion. Use the note to support a business briefing conversation.
