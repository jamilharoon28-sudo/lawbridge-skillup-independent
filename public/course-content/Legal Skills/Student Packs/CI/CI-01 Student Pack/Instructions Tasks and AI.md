### Case Simulation 01 | CI-01

## The Startup Founder With a Failed Software Launch

## Learning Outcomes

- Plan an interview that moves from open questions to focused follow-up questions.

- Identify the client’s commercial objectives before proposing legal routes.

- Separate confirmed loss, suspected loss and unsupported assumptions.

- Prepare a supervisor-ready interview note and follow-up evidence request list.

- Recognise when technical, contractual or reputational issues need escalation.

### Student Task Brief

A founder says a software supplier delivered a customer-facing platform that crashed on launch day. Your task is to prepare the interview, identify missing facts, conduct issue-focused questioning and prepare a practical follow-up note for the supervisor.

### Student Practice Tasks

Complete the following practice tasks as a trainee-style matter file review. Your answers must cite the relevant exhibit, clause, date, email, log entry or policy wording. Do not give broad commentary where a specific document reference is available.

## Detailed Practice Tasks

1. Prepare an interview plan using the founder email, supplier update and launch timeline.

2. Identify what the client wants commercially: compensation, repair, termination, renegotiation or urgent continuity.

3. Draft questions to test whether the platform failure was caused by supplier work, client configuration or third-party systems.

4. Prepare follow-up questions about contractual documents, launch testing, user complaints and financial loss.

5. Identify which points should not be promised to the client during the interview.

6. Prepare a short list of documents to request before any written advice.

7. Identify possible supervisor escalation points.

8. Draft a concise interview closing script.

9. Prepare a post-interview matter summary.

10. Reflect on which missing fact would most affect the advice.

## Working Method

- Start with the exhibit index, but do not treat it as a summary of the answer.

- Mark every clause, date, figure and disputed factual point that affects advice or drafting.

- Separate confirmed facts, disputed facts, assumptions, missing information and legal points requiring further checking.

- Keep supervisor-facing outputs concise: lead with the practical risk, then the evidence, then the next step.

### Final Student Submission Checklist

- [ ] I have identified the relevant clauses, documents and factual evidence.

- [ ] I have identified missing information and practical follow-up questions.

- [ ] I have considered commercial, ethical, client-care and reputational risks.

- [ ] I have avoided giving applicable law conclusions without checking the applicable legal framework.

- [ ] I have prepared a concise, practical and professional output.
