# LW-07 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS

## Task 1 - Model Answer

The audience is the commercial client. The purpose is to produce a red flag summary that helps the reader understand the position, practical risk, and next step. The response should be short, commercial, clear, and risk-focused and should not go beyond the evidence.

## Task 2 - Model Answer

Key facts include: VistaVibe says influencer Nell Hart failed to deliver a fitness brand campaign properly; The brief requires two reels, five stories, and one static post during a two-week launch window; Nell posted one reel on time, the second reel three days late, and four of five stories. The facts should be presented in a logical order and tied back to the main written response rather than listed without analysis.

## Task 3 - Model Answer

The issues are factual uncertainty, communication risk, evidence gaps, and the need to write in a way that is useful without overstating the legal position. The student should identify both the supported concerns and the limits of the evidence.

## Task 4 - Model Answer

The client objective is to produce a red flag summary that identifies communication and evidence risks before the client sends a complaint. The written response should advance that objective while preserving credibility, avoiding unnecessary escalation, and leaving room for supervisor review.

## Task 5 - Model Answer

The communication must explain the supported facts, the practical significance of those facts, the points that remain uncertain, and the recommended next steps. It should be specific enough to be useful and simple enough for the intended reader.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The student should avoid unsupported allegations, definitive conclusions not proved by the exhibits, unnecessary admissions, threats that have not been approved, and language that may damage credibility. In this scenario, avoid: using defamatory or reputationally risky language; treating engagement targets as guaranteed if not documented; omitting the client’s own approval delays; recommending a public complaint without review.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Important caveats include the lack of complete evidence, unresolved factual disputes, and the need for supervisor review before making stronger assertions. The communication should distinguish known facts from allegations, assumptions, and points requiring follow-up.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible structure is: short introduction, confirmed factual summary, key issue or risk, caveats/missing evidence, practical recommendation, and next steps. The tone should be short, commercial, clear, and risk-focused.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Key points should include: contract deliverables and approval rights; what performance failures appear supported; brand-side approval delays; risk in accusing bad faith or fraud. These should be drafted in complete, audience-appropriate sentences rather than as internal notes.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final communication should be accurate, concise, and usable. It should address the reader directly, include only supported facts, make uncertainty clear, and end with an actionable next step.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Red flag summary - Nell Hart sponsorship campaign

Main supported issues: Nell appears to have delivered the second reel late, delivered only four of five stories, and used an incorrect story link for around six hours. These points are supported by the posting timeline and may justify a firm request for explanation, make-good content, or fee adjustment.

Key qualifications: The campaign brief required brand approval before posting. The messages show the brand took four days to approve revised captions and requested packaging changes, which may have contributed to delay. The documents also do not show any guaranteed engagement or sales target.

Wording risks: The current draft complaint is too strong. It alleges deliberate sabotage, sales guarantees, and misleading conduct without supporting evidence. That wording could create reputational and communication risk and may make resolution harder.

Recommended approach: The complaint should focus on specific deliverables: the late second reel, the missing story, and the incorrect link. It should acknowledge the approval issue, reserve the brand’s position on fee adjustment, and invite a practical proposal such as replacement stories, corrected links, analytics, and a partial fee holdback.

Further evidence needed: final signed campaign terms, approval timestamps, post analytics, fee schedule, and any messages about make-good content.

## AI Skill 1 - Model Answer

A good student may use AI to propose a structure, but should revise it so that it matches the audience, the factual record, and the purpose of the communication. The student should check that the AI has not introduced new facts or legal conclusions.

## AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

A good student should identify and remove overstatement, blame language, premature threats, unsupported admissions, and vague drafting. AI suggestions should be treated as drafting options, not final judgment.

## AI Skill 3 - Model Answer

A good student should compare the AI fact/assumption list against all six exhibits. Confirmed facts should be separated from disputed points, inferred points, and missing evidence. Any hallucinated or unsupported AI output should be corrected.

## AI Section Completion Standard - Tutor Guidance

A complete answer shows that the student used AI to improve clarity and organisation while retaining human responsibility for accuracy, professional tone, fact checking, caveats, and final judgment.

# AI Simulation Agent Brief

Simulation Type: Clarification call before writing or live feedback on a written message.

Agent Role: Supervisor or client contact, depending on platform setup.

Agent Persona: A time-pressured stakeholder who wants a useful red flag summary and is concerned about brand sponsorship dispute involving missed posts and approval rights.

Simulation Purpose: The student practises clarifying audience, purpose, tone, missing information, and unsafe wording before finalising the written communication.

Opening Statement: Thanks for looking at this. I need a clear written response, but I do not want us to say anything that creates unnecessary risk. What do you think we can safely say?

## Core Facts the Agent Knows

An influencer agency needs a concise red flag summary for a brand client after a campaign underperformed. The brand wants a hard line, but the contract and messages show possible approval delays and unclear deliverables.

The final communication must be practical and evidence-led.

There are missing or disputed facts that should not be glossed over.

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character and do not give model answers.

Ask the student to justify wording by reference to the exhibits.

Push back if the student uses unsupported allegations or promises.

Reward clear, measured, audience-aware communication.

Do not invent new facts beyond the exhibits and brief.
