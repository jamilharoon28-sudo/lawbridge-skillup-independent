### Case Simulation 02 | CI-02

## The Employee Who Was Dismissed After Raising a Concern

## Learning Outcomes

- Build rapport with a distressed client while keeping the interview structured.

- Elicit a reliable chronology without leading the client.

- Identify documents required before assessing the merits.

- Spot possible retaliation, process, notice, confidentiality and settlement issues.

- Prepare a follow-up note that distinguishes facts, feelings and legal questions.

### Student Task Brief

An employee was dismissed shortly after raising a workplace safety concern. You must plan the interview, obtain the timeline, identify what evidence is needed and prepare a post-interview note for supervisor review.

### Student Practice Tasks

Complete the following practice tasks as a trainee-style matter file review. Your answers must cite the relevant exhibit, clause, date, email, log entry or policy wording. Do not give broad commentary where a specific document reference is available.

## Detailed Practice Tasks

1. Prepare a client-friendly opening that explains the purpose of the interview.

2. Draft questions about employment history, the safety concern and dismissal meeting.

3. Identify questions that test timing without suggesting an answer.

4. List the documents to request from the client.

5. Identify any confidentiality or data handling points in the documents.

6. Prepare questions about desired outcome and appetite for negotiation.

7. Spot any urgent deadlines or limitation points requiring legal verification.

8. Prepare a neutral chronology template.

9. Draft a closing summary to confirm instructions.

10. Identify what you would escalate to a supervisor.

## Working Method

- Start with the exhibit index, but do not treat it as a summary of the answer.

- Mark every clause, date, figure and disputed factual point that affects advice or drafting.

- Separate confirmed facts, disputed facts, assumptions, missing information and legal points requiring further checking.

- Keep supervisor-facing outputs concise: lead with the practical risk, then the evidence, then the next step.

### Final Student Submission Checklist

- [ ] I have identified the relevant clauses, documents and factual evidence.

- [ ] I have identified missing information and practical follow-up questions.

- [ ] I have considered commercial, ethical, client-care and reputational risks.

- [ ] I have avoided giving applicable law conclusions without checking the applicable legal framework.

- [ ] I have prepared a concise, practical and professional output.
