# LW-01 | TUTOR MODEL ANSWERS

## Task 1 - Model Answer

The audience is the client. The purpose is to produce a client advice email that helps the reader understand the position, practical risk, and next step. The response should be clear, calm, practical, client-friendly, and measured and should not go beyond the evidence.

## Task 2 - Model Answer

Key facts include: Amira says she paid 820 for a walnut dining table on 3 May; The order confirmation names HomeNest Online as supplier, confirms payment of 820, and describes the item as “Walnut Extendable Dining Table - 6 Seat”; Delivery booking emails show dates of 20 May, 28 May, and 5 June. The facts should be presented in a logical order and tied back to the main written response rather than listed without analysis.

## Task 3 - Model Answer

The issues are factual uncertainty, communication risk, evidence gaps, and the need to write in a way that is useful without overstating the legal position. The student should identify both the supported concerns and the limits of the evidence.

## Task 4 - Model Answer

The client objective is to obtain a refund or suitable replacement without making unsupported allegations or escalating prematurely. The written response should advance that objective while preserving credibility, avoiding unnecessary escalation, and leaving room for supervisor review.

## Task 5 - Model Answer

The communication must explain the supported facts, the practical significance of those facts, the points that remain uncertain, and the recommended next steps. It should be specific enough to be useful and simple enough for the intended reader.

## Task 6 - Model Answer

The student should avoid unsupported allegations, definitive conclusions not proved by the exhibits, unnecessary admissions, threats that have not been approved, and language that may damage credibility. In this scenario, avoid: saying the retailer acted fraudulently or deliberately; threatening public posts or legal action as a first response; stating that all losses are recoverable without evidence; ignoring the retailer offer to inspect or replace the item.

## Task 7 - Model Answer

Important caveats include the lack of complete evidence, unresolved factual disputes, and the need for supervisor review before making stronger assertions. The communication should distinguish known facts from allegations, assumptions, and points requiring follow-up.

## Task 8 - Model Answer

A sensible structure is: short introduction, confirmed factual summary, key issue or risk, caveats/missing evidence, practical recommendation, and next steps. The tone should be clear, calm, practical, client-friendly, and measured.

## Task 9 - Model Answer

Key points should include: what facts are supported by the order confirmation, delivery log, and retailer messages; what Amira can safely say about delay, inconvenience, and the damaged substitute; what information is missing before stronger allegations are made; a proportionate next step: a concise written request for refund or replacement with a deadline for response. These should be drafted in complete, audience-appropriate sentences rather than as internal notes.

## Task 10 - Model Answer

The final communication should be accurate, concise, and usable. It should address the reader directly, include only supported facts, make uncertainty clear, and end with an actionable next step.

# Main Practical Task - Full Model Answer

Dear Amira,

Thank you for sending the order confirmation, delivery emails, the retailer response, and your draft reply. Based on the documents available, the safest position is to write back in a firm but measured way. The documents support that you paid 820 for the dining table, that the expected delivery timing was missed more than once, and that the item presented on 5 June appeared damaged. They also show that HomeNest has offered a replacement within 21 days or a price reduction, and that it has asked for photos and the courier report before confirming any refund.

I would avoid describing the retailer’s conduct as deliberate fraud or saying that all losses are recoverable at this stage. The documents do not yet prove deliberate conduct, and we have not reviewed jurisdiction-specific consumer rules. Those points could distract from the stronger practical request.

A sensible next reply would: (1) state the order details and the three delivery issues; (2) explain that the item tendered on 5 June was visibly damaged and was refused; (3) attach any delivery photographs you have; (4) ask HomeNest to confirm whether it will provide a full refund or an undamaged replacement by a clear date; and (5) reserve your position on any further losses until the retailer responds.

Please send any photographs, courier texts, or notes of calls with HomeNest before the final email is sent. We can then help you keep the wording accurate, firm, and proportionate.

Kind regards,
[Name]

## AI Skill 1 - Model Answer

A good student may use AI to propose a structure, but should revise it so that it matches the audience, the factual record, and the purpose of the communication. The student should check that the AI has not introduced new facts or legal conclusions.

## AI Skill 2 - Model Answer

A good student should identify and remove overstatement, blame language, premature threats, unsupported admissions, and vague drafting. AI suggestions should be treated as drafting options, not final judgment.

## AI Skill 3 - Model Answer

A good student should compare the AI fact/assumption list against all six exhibits. Confirmed facts should be separated from disputed points, inferred points, and missing evidence. Any hallucinated or unsupported AI output should be corrected.

## AI Section Completion Standard - Tutor Guidance

A complete answer shows that the student used AI to improve clarity and organisation while retaining human responsibility for accuracy, professional tone, fact checking, caveats, and final judgment.

# AI Simulation Agent Brief

Simulation Type: Clarification call before writing or live feedback on a written message.

Agent Role: Supervisor or client contact, depending on platform setup.

Agent Persona: A time-pressured stakeholder who wants a useful client advice email and is concerned about an online furniture order cancelled after repeated delivery failures.

Simulation Purpose: The student practises clarifying audience, purpose, tone, missing information, and unsafe wording before finalising the written communication.

Opening Statement: Thanks for looking at this. I need a clear written response, but I do not want us to say anything that creates unnecessary risk. What do you think we can safely say?

## Core Facts the Agent Knows

Amira ordered a dining table from HomeNest Online for a new flat. The delivery was rearranged three times, a damaged table was offered as a substitute, and Amira now wants a clear written explanation of her options before she replies to the retailer. She has drafted an angry email but the evidence does not yet support every allegation she wants to make.

The final communication must be practical and evidence-led.

There are missing or disputed facts that should not be glossed over.

## Behaviour Rules

Stay in character and do not give model answers.

Ask the student to justify wording by reference to the exhibits.

Push back if the student uses unsupported allegations or promises.

Reward clear, measured, audience-aware communication.

Do not invent new facts beyond the exhibits and brief.
