**Case Simulation 20 \| DR-02**

**Reviewing a Settlement Pack Before Signature**

**Learning Outcomes**

> **•** Review signature documents carefully.
>
> **•** Check parties, dates, payment terms and conditions.
>
> **•** Identify ambiguity in settlement obligations.
>
> **•** Spot confidentiality and release issues.
>
> **•** Prepare a practical pre-signature checklist.

**Student Task Brief**

A small business is about to sign a settlement agreement after a service dispute. The client wants a quick check of the draft agreement, payment schedule and confidentiality wording.

**Student Practice Tasks**

Complete the following practice tasks as a trainee-style matter file review. Your answers must cite the relevant exhibit, clause, date, email, log entry or policy wording. Do not give broad commentary where a specific document reference is available.

**Detailed Practice Tasks**

> **01** Check the parties and defined terms.
>
> **02** Review payment amount, due date and bank details.
>
> **03** Identify conditions before settlement becomes binding.
>
> **04** Review release wording and confidentiality wording.
>
> **05** Check signature blocks and authority questions.
>
> **06** Identify inconsistent dates or amounts.
>
> **07** Prepare a supervisor issue list.
>
> **08** Prepare client clarification questions.
>
> **09** Reflect on why small drafting details can have commercial impact.

**Working Method**

> **•** Start with the exhibit index, but do not treat it as a summary of the answer.
>
> **•** Mark every clause, date, figure and disputed factual point that affects advice or drafting.
>
> **•** Separate confirmed facts, disputed facts, assumptions, missing information and legal points requiring further checking.
>
> **•** Keep supervisor-facing outputs concise: lead with the practical risk, then the evidence, then the next step.

**Final Student Submission Checklist**

> ☐ I have used plain English and avoided unnecessary jargon.
>
> ☐ I have identified relevant facts and documents.
>
> ☐ I have separated confirmed facts from assumptions, disputes and missing information.
>
> ☐ I have considered client care, professionalism and commercial context where relevant.
>
> ☐ I have avoided giving jurisdiction-specific legal advice.
>
> ☐ I have prepared a concise, practical and professional output.
