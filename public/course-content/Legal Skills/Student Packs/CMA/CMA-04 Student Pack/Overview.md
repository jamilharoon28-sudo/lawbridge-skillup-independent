*Analysing a Departing Employee Client List Dispute*

**Scenario Title** Analysing a Departing Employee Client List Dispute

**Industry / Practice Area** Employment / Professional Services / Confidential Information

**Difficulty** Intermediate

**Scenario Type** Case and matter analysis simulation

# Scenario Background

Pinnacle Tax Advisory LLP has asked for help with possible misuse of client contact information after a senior adviser resigned. Nina Shah, Managing Partner, wants the legal team to help prepare an issue, evidence and action analysis before any formal letter is sent. A major client has received a pitch from the former employee and the managing partner wants urgent action. The available file is incomplete and contains conflicting accounts, so the student must analyse the matter carefully before any external communication or recommendation is made.

Central tension: The former employee denies taking any confidential list, but internal logs show downloads shortly before resignation. The matter cannot be resolved by accepting either side's account without further evidence.

# Student Role

You are acting as a junior legal professional in the team. You report to Ethan Cole. You are not expected to make final strategic decisions or send external correspondence. Your task is to analyse the matter file, organise the evidence, identify strengths and weaknesses, and prepare a clear note for supervisor review.

# Documents / Information Available

1. Exhibit 1 - File Opening Note and Client Objective
1. Exhibit 2 - Chronology of Key Events
1. Exhibit 3 - Key Correspondence Extract
1. Exhibit 4 - Relevant Agreement or Policy Extract
1. Exhibit 5 - Conflicting Evidence Summary
1. Exhibit 6 - Supervisor Instruction and Risk Note

# Core Skill Task

Analyse a realistic matter file, identify the key issues and evidence gaps, and prepare a practical case/matter analysis note for a supervising solicitor or matter lead.

# Main Practical Task

Prepare a concise case/matter analysis note for Ethan Cole. Your note should summarise the client objective, key facts, issue list, strengths, weaknesses, evidence gaps, communication risks, recommended next steps, and decisions requiring supervisor input.
