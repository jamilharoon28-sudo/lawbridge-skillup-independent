### Tutor Use Note

Use this guide alongside the matching student pack and separate exhibit documents.

The model answers indicate the standard, structure and professional judgment expected. They are not intended to be memorised.

Reward careful use of the file, clear caveats, practical next steps and thoughtful escalation.

Do not reward confident conclusions unsupported by exhibits.

The simulation is applicable legal framework-neutral. Students should identify points requiring verification rather than assume a jurisdiction.

## Matter Snapshot for Tutors

A company used an AI design tool to create a campaign image. A third party alleges copying. You must draft a client advice email explaining the issue, evidence required, immediate response and future risk controls.

## Required Output Guidance

Client advice email

Clear explanation of the complaint, risk, evidence needed, options and next steps.

Evidence standard: Must be understandable to a non-lawyer.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Evidence schedule

Documents required from marketing, design, AI tool provider and complainant.

Evidence standard: Each item must explain why it matters.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

Communication risk note

What the client can say externally and what should be avoided.

Evidence standard: Must protect position and manage reputational risk.

Check whether the answer is practical, concise, caveated and grounded in the documents.

> AI Extension Model Answers - Three Scenario Questions

> Tutor assessment focus Tutors should assess only three AI questions. Strong answers must be grounded in LW-01 - Writing a Client Advice Email After an AI Tool Dispute and the matching exhibit bundle, not generic AI risk language.

> - AI Task 1 - Human Skill First Strong answer identifies three priority issues from the exhibit bundle, explains why they matter for Legal Writing, separates confirmed facts from gaps, and states what must be checked or escalated before AI is used.

> - AI Task 2 - AI Tool Usage and Critique Strong answer gives one safe prompt limited to LW-01 - Writing a Client Advice Email After an AI Tool Dispute; asks for structure, critique or drafting support only; requires primary practical output, risk or issue list, reflection or improvement note; prohibits invented facts or assumed law; and explains how the output will be checked against exhibits and source reliability.

> - AI Task 3 - Judgment and AI Supervision Under Pressure Strong answer explains what the student would accept, change or reject from AI output; keeps caveats visible; identifies missing evidence and approval points; and produces a supervisor-ready position grounded in the scenario.
